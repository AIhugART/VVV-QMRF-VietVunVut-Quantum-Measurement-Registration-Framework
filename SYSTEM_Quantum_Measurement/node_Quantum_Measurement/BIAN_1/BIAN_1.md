# BIAN-1 — Quantum Appearance

## Source Mapping

### Buddhist Epistemology: Aabhaasa — Appearance in cognition / phenomenal presentation

Aabhaasa is the appearance of an object within consciousness. It is not the external object itself, nor the pure particular. It is how the object presents to the knower. The distinction between aabhaasa and the object it represents is foundational to Pramaanavada.

### Quantum Measurement: BIAN-1 — Quantum Appearance

BIAN-1 denotes the result of a measurement as it presents to an observer, prior to and separate from any claim about the underlying state of the system that produced it.

Quantum mechanics does not formally distinguish between the appearance of a result and the ontological status of the system that produced it.

## Structural Gap

Quantum Measurement lacks a formal category for the appearance-reality distinction at the epistemic level.

## Structural Summary Entry

BIAN-1: Quantum Appearance corresponds to Aabhaasa and marks the absence of an appearance-reality distinction in Quantum Measurement formalism.

## Processing Notes

- BIAN-1 is an original structural label denoting a concept present in Buddhist Epistemology but absent in Quantum Measurement formalism.
- This mapping is epistemological only. It does not make metaphysical, soteriological, cosmological, historical, or intentional-correlation claims.
- The Buddhist ground system is the Pramaanavada tradition of Dignaga and Dharmakirti.
- The Quantum Measurement framework referenced is standard Copenhagen-adjacent formalism, with relevant notes on relational QM and QBism.
