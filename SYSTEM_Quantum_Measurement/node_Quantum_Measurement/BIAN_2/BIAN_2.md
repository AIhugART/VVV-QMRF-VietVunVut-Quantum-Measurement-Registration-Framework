## Source Mapping

### Buddhist Epistemology: Savikalpaka Pratyaksa — Conceptual perception / perception with conceptual overlay

Savikalpaka pratyaksa is perception that has already been processed through conceptual categories. It is still perceptual in origin but now carries linguistic and categorical structure. It is valid but secondary to nirvikalpaka pratyaksa (non-conceptual perception).

In the Pramaanavada framework of Dignaga and Dharmakirti, savikalpaka pratyaksa occupies a distinct epistemic stratum: it is the cognitive event where raw sensory data (apprehended by nirvikalpaka pratyaksa) has been integrated with conceptual categories, linguistic labels, and prior knowledge structures. It is not pure inference (anumaana) because it retains direct perceptual origin, but it is not pure perception either because it carries conceptual overlay.

Key characteristics:
- **Perceptual origin**: Still grounded in direct sensory contact with the particular (svalaksana)
- **Conceptual overlay**: Processed through linguistic categories, cognitive schemas, and prior knowledge
- **Secondary validity**: Epistemically valid but derivative of nirvikalpaka pratyaksa
- **Linguistic structure**: Bears the imprint of language and categorical thought
- **Functional necessity**: Required for practical action and communication about perception

### Quantum Measurement: BIAN-2 — Interpreted Measurement

BIAN-2 denotes the measurement outcome after it has been embedded in a theoretical framework: assigned a physical meaning, a unit, a variable name, a place in a model.

In current Quantum Measurement formalism, when a measurement is performed, the eigenvalue readout (pratyaksa-level) is immediately and seamlessly integrated into a theoretical interpretation. The raw numerical result — say, a detector click at position x — is not formally distinguished from the interpreted result — "the electron was measured at position x = 3.7 nm relative to the reference frame." The assignment of units, variable names, physical meaning, and model placement happens without any formal demarcation between the raw output and the interpreted output.

Quantum Mechanics does not formally distinguish this interpreted measurement from the raw eigenvalue readout. The theory proceeds as if the output of measurement is simultaneously raw data and interpreted data, without recognizing the transformation that occurs between these two epistemic levels.

## Structural Gap

**Quantum Measurement has no formal distinction between raw eigenvalue readout and theoretically interpreted output.**

This gap has specific manifestations:

1. **No unit-assignment operator**: There is no formal operation in QM that takes a raw measurement outcome and assigns it a physical unit (eV, nm, etc.). Units are assumed a priori in the experimental setup.

2. **No variable-naming formalism**: The assignment of variable names (position, momentum, spin-component) is done at the experimental design stage, not formally theorized as part of the measurement process.

3. **No model-placement operation**: The embedding of a measurement outcome into a theoretical model (e.g., "this click corresponds to the electron in the nth energy level of the hydrogen atom") is not formalized. It is considered part of experimental interpretation, not part of the quantum formalism itself.

4. **No raw-to-interpreted transformation**: There is no mathematical operator or formal procedure that represents the transition from a raw numerical output to a physically meaningful interpreted result. The two are treated as identical in the formalism.

5. **Collapse of epistemic layers**: By not distinguishing raw from interpreted output, QM collapses two distinct epistemic strata into one. This creates ambiguity about what exactly a measurement outcome *is* — a raw detector event or an interpreted physical fact.

---

## Structural Summary Entry

BIAN-2: Interpreted Measurement corresponds to Savikalpaka Pratyaksa and marks the absence of a formal raw-output-versus-interpreted-output distinction in Quantum Measurement formalism.

## Epistemic Analysis

### The Three-Layer Model

Buddhist Epistemology provides a three-layer model of cognitive processing that QM lacks:

| Layer | Buddhist Epistemology | Quantum Measurement | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-conceptual | Nirvikalpaka Pratyaksa | BIAN-3 (Pre-Theoretical Quantum Event) | Gap |
| Raw perceptual | Pratyaksa | Eigenvalue Readout | Correspondence |
| Conceptually processed | Savikalpaka Pratyaksa | BIAN-2 (Interpreted Measurement) | Gap |

The eigenvalue readout sits at the middle layer. BIAN-2 identifies the missing third layer: the interpreted measurement that carries conceptual and theoretical structure.

### Relation to Apoha (Exclusion)

Savikalpaka pratyaksa operates through the mechanism of apoha (exclusion). When a measurement outcome is interpreted as "the electron is at position x," this interpretation works by excluding all other positions — the electron is at x because it is not at non-x. This apoha-like operation of conceptual exclusion is implicit in quantum measurement interpretation but not formally theorized.

### Relation to Anumaana (Inference)

Interpreted measurement straddles the boundary between perception (pratyaksa) and inference (anumaana). The raw eigenvalue is perceptual; the interpreted result carries inferential structure because it embeds the particular in a universal framework (theoretical model). Buddhist Epistemology recognizes this hybrid nature explicitly through savikalpaka pratyaksa; QM does not.

## Processing Notes

- BIAN-2 is an original structural label denoting a concept present in Buddhist Epistemology but absent in Quantum Measurement formalism.
- This mapping is epistemological only. It does not make metaphysical, soteriological, cosmological, historical, or intentional-correlation claims.
- The Buddhist ground system is the Pramaanavada tradition of Dignaga and Dharmakirti.
- The Quantum Measurement framework referenced is standard Copenhagen-adjacent formalism, with relevant notes on relational QM (Rovelli) and QBism (Fuchs-Schack) where applicable.
- Savikalpaka pratyaksa is sometimes translated as "determinate perception" or "conceptual perception" in secondary literature. Both translations are valid.
- The distinction between nirvikalpaka (BIAN-3) and savikalpaka (BIAN-2) pratyaksa is one of the most rigorously analyzed distinctions in Pramaanavada. Its absence in QM formalism represents a significant structural asymmetry.
- All Sanskrit terms are written in simplified ASCII romanization without diacritics for parsing stability.
