## Mapping Confidence Rules

These rules define the confidence grammar for cross-domain correspondences in this system mapping. They are adapted from the auxiliary RCA mapping table but applied here as system-level interpretation rules, not as replacements for the node/edge structure.

### Analogy-not-equivalence warning

Cross-domain correspondences in this document are structural analogies only. They do not assert doctrinal, physical, historical, or ontological equivalence between Buddhist Epistemology and Quantum Measurement. A strong correspondence means high mapping confidence within the comparison grammar; it does not mean that the two systems describe the same entity, mechanism, or truth-claim.

- `Strong` means close structural or functional analogy between a Buddhist Epistemology concept and a Quantum Measurement concept.
- `Medium` means a clear structural analogy without formal equivalence.
- `BIAN-n` means a canonical structural gap listed in the BIAN Index of this document. BIAN labels identify concepts present in Buddhist Epistemology but absent from Quantum Measurement formalism, except for explicitly marked reverse gaps such as BIAN-10.
- Cross-domain mappings are structural analogies only, not doctrinal, physical, historical, or ontological equivalences.

Weak mappings and forced anchors are retrieval aids from auxiliary RCA tables. They preserve line-by-line coverage without promoting weak comparison into system ontology.

- `Weak` means the nearest available comparison only; weak mappings are useful for retrieval or commentary but should not be treated as hard ontology edges.
- Forced 1:1 anchors from auxiliary RCA tables are not canonical BIAN labels unless they correspond to numbered BIAN entries in this document.
- RCA evidence can support a correspondence, but it cannot create a new node, edge, BIAN label, or equivalence claim by itself.

### Evidence/provenance discipline

Evidence and provenance from auxiliary RCA tables may support retrieval and auditing, but the node/edge structure and BIAN Index in this document remain canonical.

# Four Interpretive Lenses — Secondary Epistemological Synthesis

The six-tier Pramāṇavāda mapping remains the primary structure of this document. The following four lenses are used only as secondary organizing devices for paper-level synthesis. They are not claimed as formal axioms of Pramāṇavāda, nor as ontological claims about quantum mechanics. Their function is comparative and epistemological: they identify structural parallels in how cognition, evidence, objecthood, and measurement are organized.

| # | Interpretive lens | Pali / Sanskrit | Epistemological principle | QM interpretive domain |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Dependent Arising | Paṭiccasamuppāda (Pāli) / Pratītyasamutpāda (Skt.) | No epistemic state is treated as self-standing; cognition and objecthood are analyzed through conditions, relations, and causal dependence. | In table-supported QM language, the quantum state is treated here within a specified preparation and measurement context. The wavefunction is used here as a structure of possible outcomes within that context. |
| 2 | Emptiness | Suññatā (Pāli) / Śūnyatā (Skt.) | No epistemic object has fixed intrinsic identity independent of conditions; apparent properties are analyzed as context-dependent imputations. | Measurement outcomes for spin or position are context-dependent with respect to the chosen observable and measurement arrangement. Bell-type constraints on local hidden-variable accounts support this negative structural parallel. |
| 3 | Cognitive Reflexivity / Representational Appearance | Vijñaptimātra (Skt., used narrowly) / Svasaṃvedana (Skt.) | The object-as-known is inseparable from its mode of cognitive appearance. This is used as an epistemological claim about representation and reflexive certification, not as a metaphysical claim that consciousness creates reality. | Quantum measurement does not reveal a pre-given value independently of measurement context. In the table-supported state-update account, collapse may be treated operationally as an update in the description of possible outcomes; standard QM itself does not settle the ontological status of collapse. |
| 4 | Two Descriptive Registers | Dvisatya (Skt.) | Conventional truth (saṃvṛtisat) and ultimate truth (paramārthasat) are used here only as two registers of cognition and description, not as a metaphysical claim about levels of reality. | The quantum/classical distinction is used as an analogy for a shift between formal/context-dependent description and conventional communicable description. |

# TIER 1 — Sources of Valid Cognition (Pramāṇa Sources)

## T1.01 — Pramāṇa (Valid cognition / Valid epistemic instrument)

**Node:** N_BE_00001 — Pramāṇa (Skt.). Category: Foundation.
**Definition:** A reliable source of knowledge; the means by which correct cognition is attained. Structured with four integrated components: means of knowing (pramāṇa), valid cognition (pramā), object to be known (prameya), and resultant cognition (pramāṇaphala).

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00036: N_BE_00030 (Pramāṇabhūta) → N_BE_00001 — is epithet of (Foundational)

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00001: N_BE_00001 → N_BE_00002 (Pratyakṣa) — includes (Hierarchical)
- ED_BE_00002: N_BE_00001 → N_BE_00003 (Anumāna) — includes (Hierarchical)
- ED_BE_00003: N_BE_00001 → N_BE_00005 (Prameya) — has as object (Structural)
- ED_BE_00004: N_BE_00001 → pramāṇaphala — produces (Structural)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The foundational category of Pramāṇavāda. A cognition qualifies as pramāṇa if it is non-deceptive (avisaṃvādin) with respect to its object and causally efficacious in guiding action toward that object. Validity is relational and functional, not intrinsic to any object or subject alone. Pramāṇa is the condition-event under which knowledge is certified. Dignāga identifies two pramāṇas: pratyakṣa and anumāna. Dharmakīrti refines and defends both against Mīmāṃsaka and Nyāya objections.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Observable / Hermitian operator, with generalized measurement represented by POVM elements and Kraus operators. A Hermitian operator defines an observable with a real spectrum; POVM/Kraus formalism defines outcome statistics and state updates for generalized measurement. Validity is defined by the formal measurement structure, not by relation to a cognitive agent.
- **Correspondence type:** Structural parallel with divergence. Both define validity at the level of the act-instrument rather than the object or subject. Divergence: Pramāṇa formally requires a pramātṛ (knower) as one of its constitutive conditions. Observable and generalized-measurement formalisms are defined without reference to any observer. This divergence is foundational and propagates through all subsequent BIAN gaps.

## T1.02 — Pratyakṣa (Direct perception / Non-inferential cognition)

**Node:** N_BE_00002 — Pratyakṣa (Skt.). Category: Source of knowledge.
**Definition:** Immediate cognition through the senses, prior to inference or conceptual overlay. Dignāga defines it as "perception (pratyakṣam) is devoid of mental construction (kalpanāpodham)." It apprehends the particular (svalakṣaṇa) as pure sensation without conceptual structure.

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00001: N_BE_00001 (Pramāṇa) → N_BE_00002 — includes (Hierarchical)
- ED_BE_00018: N_BE_00010 (Mānasa pratyakṣa) → N_BE_00002 — is subtype of (Hierarchical)
- ED_BE_00019: N_BE_00011 (Svasaṃvedana) → N_BE_00002 — is subtype of (Hierarchical)
- ED_BE_00021: N_BE_00012 (Yogipratyakṣa) → N_BE_00002 — is subtype of (Hierarchical)

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00005: N_BE_00002 → N_BE_00013 (Svalakṣaṇa) — apprehends (Epistemic)
- ED_BE_00038: N_BE_00002 → N_BE_00008 (Vikalpa) — is free from (Exclusionary)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** One of the two valid pramāṇas in Dignāga-Dharmakīrti. Defined negatively: cognition free from kalpanā (conceptual construction) and abhrānta (error). Pratyakṣa apprehends the svalakṣaṇa (unique particular) directly, without inferential mediation. It is momentary and non-repeatable in the strict sense. Dignāga distinguishes four subtypes (see T1.04 through T1.07).
- **Quantum Measurement:** Eigenvalue readout. The singular, definite numerical outcome produced by a projective measurement on a quantum system. Non-inferential in the sense that it is the terminal output, not derived from prior outcomes. Produced at a specific moment; non-repeatable in the sense that the post-measurement state differs from the pre-measurement state.
- **Correspondence type:** Strong parallel. Both are the terminal, non-inferential epistemic event. Divergence: Pratyakṣa is non-conceptual by definition; an eigenvalue readout is already a number — a symbolic, conceptual object. The eigenvalue has been processed through mathematical structure before it reaches any observer. This means no QM concept maps to pratyakṣa at its deepest level (see BIAN-7).

## T1.03 — Anumāna (Inference / Inferential cognition)

**Node:** N_BE_00003 — Anumāna (Skt.). Category: Source of knowledge.
**Definition:** Indirect knowledge obtained through logical reasoning based on a sign (liṅga). Vasubandhu defines it as "the observation of an object not occurring without [the probandum] for someone who knows that." It operates on the universal (sāmānyalakṣaṇa).

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00002: N_BE_00001 (Pramāṇa) → N_BE_00003 — includes (Hierarchical)
- ED_BE_00016: N_BE_00015 (Apoha) → N_BE_00003 — is linguistic variant of (Logical)
- ED_BE_00017: N_BE_00004 (Āgama) → N_BE_00003 — reduced to (Hierarchical)
- ED_BE_00023: N_BE_00027 (Svārthānumāna) → N_BE_00003 — is subtype of (Hierarchical)
- ED_BE_00024: N_BE_00028 (Parārthānumāna) → N_BE_00003 — is subtype of (Hierarchical)

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00006: N_BE_00003 → N_BE_00014 (Sāmānyalakṣaṇa) — apprehends (Epistemic)
- ED_BE_00007: N_BE_00003 → N_BE_00016 (Liṅga) — operates through (Logical)
- ED_BE_00008: N_BE_00003 → N_BE_00018 (Trairūpya) — validated by (Logical)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Second valid pramāṇa. Valid cognition of an object not directly present, derived from a logical mark (liṅga) via invariant pervasion (vyāpti). Anumāna does not contact its object directly; it constructs knowledge from relational structure. It operates on sāmānyalakṣaṇa (conceptual universals), not svalakṣaṇa. Dignāga distinguishes svārtha anumāna (for oneself) and parārtha anumāna (for others / publicly demonstrable). Full treatment of anumāna architecture in Tier 5.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Wave function ψ. A probability amplitude function that encodes the relational structure of possible outcomes without directly representing any particular outcome. All predictions derived from ψ are inferential relative to actual measurement events. ψ is not observed directly; it is inferred from statistical patterns.
- **Correspondence type:** Structural parallel. Both are indirect, mediated representations of what is not directly present. Both are the structural vehicle for prediction.

## T1.04 — Indriyajñāna (Sense perception)

**Node:** Subdivision of N_BE_00002 (Pratyakṣa). Not a separate node.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Perception arising from causal contact between a sense faculty (indriya) and its appropriate object. The primary, most basic form of pratyakṣa. Produced by the object through the sense organ as causal chain.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Detector response. The physical signal produced in a measurement apparatus upon direct interaction with a quantum system: photon count, current spike, pointer deflection, ionization track. The causal-physical interface between system and apparatus.
- **Correspondence type:** Moderate parallel. Both are the causal-physical interface event. Divergence: Indriyajñāna is attributed to a faculty of a cognitive agent. Detector response is a physical process attributed to an apparatus. The apparatus has no cognitive faculty; the faculty has no physical extension.

## T1.05 — Mānasapratyakṣa (Mental perception)

**Node:** N_BE_00010 — Mānasa pratyakṣa (Skt.). Category: Type of consciousness.
**Definition:** Inner cognition through mental consciousness, treated here as post-sensory mental perception within the Pramāṇavāda mapping. Some Yogācāra contexts relate this to mental consciousness within broader vijñāna taxonomies, but those ontological claims are outside the present scope.

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00018: N_BE_00010 → N_BE_00002 (Pratyakṣa) — is subtype of (Hierarchical)
- ED_BE_00020: N_BE_00010 → N_BE_00011 (Svasaṃvedana) — entails (Structural)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Perception arising in manas (mind-faculty) immediately following indriyajñāna. Apprehends the mental image (ākāra) of the sense object rather than the external object directly. Still non-conceptual (free of kalpanā) but one causal step removed from direct sense contact.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-1 — Post-Detection Internal Representational State. The internal state of an observer or apparatus system after a detector event fires but before any symbolic encoding, recording, or storage of the result. QM moves directly from physical interaction to symbolic output value. The intermediate stratum — between raw physical signal and registered number — is unmodeled.
- **Structural gap:** QM has no formal account of the representational transition between physical detection and symbolic readout. The gap between click and number is outside the formalism.

## T1.06 — Svasaṃvedana (Self-awareness / Reflexive cognition)

**Node:** N_BE_00011 — Svasaṃvedana (Skt.). Category: Type of perception.
**Definition:** [uncertain] The reflexive dimension by which a cognition is aware of its own occurrence. In this document, svasaṃvedana is used as a principle of epistemic self-certification and regress-stopping, not as a general metaphysical thesis about the existence of external objects.

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00020: N_BE_00010 (Mānasa pratyakṣa) → N_BE_00011 — entails (Structural) [Note: uncertain]

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00019: N_BE_00011 → N_BE_00002 (Pratyakṣa) — is subtype of (Hierarchical) [Note: uncertain]

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Every cognition is simultaneously aware of itself. Cognition of blue is also, non-inferentially, an awareness of the cognizing-of-blue. This is a distinct pramāṇa for Dharmakīrti: self-luminous (svaprakāśa), not requiring a second-order act to register the first-order act. Svasaṃvedana is the structural grounding of the entire Pramāṇavāda system: it stops the regress of meta-cognitions and self-certifies every valid cognition without external verification. Its implications at the system level are treated in T2.05, T6.01, and T6.02.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-2 — Observer Self-Reference / Reflexive Cognition of Measurement Act. QM has no formal account of an observer's cognition of its own measurement act. A measurement is modeled as a physical interaction between system and apparatus. There is no formalism for the registering agent being simultaneously aware of its own registering. From this mapping's perspective, this gap helps explain why the von Neumann chain remains structurally salient: without a self-certifying epistemic layer, the registration chain has no internal epistemic stopping principle.
- **Structural gap:** This is the deepest single asymmetry in the mapping. Svasaṃvedana is not one feature among others in Pramāṇavāda; it is the system's foundational self-certification principle. The published QM table has no direct equivalent, so the comparison remains an epistemological diagnosis rather than a final judgment on standard QM.

## T1.07 — Yogijñāna (Yogic direct perception)

**Node:** N_BE_00012 — Yogipratyakṣa (Skt.). Category: Type of perception.
**Definition:** [uncertain] Direct, supra-ordinary perception attributed to cultivated epistemic capacity. Ultimate-object and soteriological claims are outside the present scope unless used only as structural features of epistemic access.

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00021: N_BE_00012 → N_BE_00002 (Pratyakṣa) — is subtype of (Hierarchical) [Note: uncertain]
- ED_BE_00022: N_BE_00012 → N_BE_00025 (Śūnyatā) — realizes (Epistemic) [Note: uncertain]

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Non-inferential, non-conceptual cognition achieved through sustained meditative cultivation (bhāvanā). Accesses objects or levels of reality normally beyond sensory reach. Debated within Pramāṇavāda but included by Dharmakīrti as a limiting case of pratyakṣa, achieved when a cognitive agent reaches a qualitatively different epistemic faculty through practice.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-3 — Limit-Case Observation by Different Faculty. QM has no formal category for observation that operates through a qualitatively different faculty or mechanism than standard measurement. All observers are formally equivalent in QM: any physical system that interacts with a quantum system and produces a correlated output counts as a measurement apparatus. There is no spectrum of observer types based on cultivated capacity.
- **Structural gap:** Buddhist Epistemology has a spectrum of observer types distinguished by epistemic faculty. QM has one observer type, undefined.

## T1.08 — Svārtha anumāna (Inference for oneself)

**Node:** N_BE_00027 — Svārthānumāna (Skt.). Category: Logic.
**Definition:** "Inference for oneself" — a private inferential cognition based on observation of an evidential sign (liṅga) fulfilling the three conditions (trairūpya). Endowed with personal psychological characteristics; the process begins with perceptible observation (darśana) of the sign.

**Inbound edges:**

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Inference conducted by a cognitive agent for its own epistemic purposes. The full syllogistic structure need not be made explicit. The primary, psychologically real form of inference. Its validity conditions are the same as parārtha anumāna but its expression is internal.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Private theoretical calculation. A physicist's internal derivation of a predicted outcome from ψ before experiment. The reasoning process that leads from known wave function to expected eigenvalue distribution. Not formalized as a distinct category in QM.
- **Correspondence type:** Weak functional parallel. Not a formal QM category; recognized only as informal scientific practice.

## T1.09 — Parārtha anumāna (Inference for others / Public demonstration)

**Node:** N_BE_00028 — Parārthānumāna (Skt.). Category: Logic.
**Definition:** "Inference for others" — syllogistic reasoning communicating new knowledge to another person. Its purpose is "to produce in the opponent the same kind of ascertainment that is obtained by the proponent through an inference (svārthānumāna)." Taken in a metaphorical sense (upacāra).

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00025: N_BE_00027 (Svārthānumāna) → N_BE_00028 — grounds (Logical)

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00024: N_BE_00028 → N_BE_00003 (Anumāna) — is subtype of (Hierarchical)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Inference made explicit in syllogistic form (prayoga) for the purpose of demonstrating a conclusion to another cognitive agent. Requires full articulation of pakṣa (subject), hetu (reason / liṅga), and dṛṣṭānta (example). The public, communicable, and contestable form of inference.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Published experimental result with full measurement protocol. The public, reproducible form of a quantum measurement including apparatus specification, preparation procedure, statistical output, and error bars. Subject to peer scrutiny and replication.
- **Correspondence type:** Functional parallel at the level of scientific communication norms. Both are the public, explicit, contestable form of inference-with-evidence.

# TIER 2 — Structure of Cognition (Internal Architecture)

## T2.01 — Ākāra (Representational form / Intentional content)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT. Implicit in the structure of cognition.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The form that an object takes within cognition. Cognition does not contact the external object directly but apprehends its ākāra (intentional image). The Sākāra-Nirākāra debate: Sākāra schools (including Dharmakīrti) hold that ākāra is a real feature of cognition; Nirākāra schools deny it. On the Sākāra reading, the ākāra is the proximate object of every cognition. The gap between ākāra and the external object (artha) is foundational.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-4 — Measurement Representation / Internal Encoding Structure. QM specifies the output value of a measurement (the eigenvalue) but not the form in which that output is represented within any cognizing or registering system. The formalism ends at the number; what the number means in the observer's representational structure is outside the theory.
- **Structural gap:** Buddhist Epistemology has an explicit theory of representational form (ākāra) as the proximate object of every cognition. QM has no equivalent. It specifies output; it does not specify how output is internally represented.

---

## T2.02 — Viṣaya (Object domain / Intentional object)

**Node:** N_BE_00005 — Prameya (Skt.). Category: Foundation.
**Definition:** The reality that serves as the object of valid knowledge. Dignāga maintains exclusive duality: svalakṣaṇa as the object of perception and sāmānyalakṣaṇa as the object of inference.

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00003: N_BE_00001 (Pramāṇa) → N_BE_00005 — has as object (Structural)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The domain to which a cognition is directed. Distinguished from artha (the real external causal ground): viṣaya is the epistemic object as it figures in the cognitive act; artha is what causally produces the ākāra. The two are structurally distinct. Erroneous cognition has a viṣaya but its artha is not as the viṣaya presents it.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Observable. The physical quantity to which a measurement is directed: position, momentum, spin, energy. Distinguished from the actual quantum state of the system, which may not have a definite value for that observable prior to measurement. The observable is the epistemic target; the state is the causal ground.
- **Correspondence type:** Strong structural parallel. The viṣaya-artha distinction maps directly onto the observable-state distinction. Both distinguish the epistemic target from the causal ground.

---

## T2.03 — Phala (Result of cognition / Epistemic fruit)

**Node:** Sub-component of N_BE_00001 (Pramāṇa). Pramāṇaphala is the fourth component.

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00004: N_BE_00001 (Pramāṇa) → pramāṇaphala — produces (Structural)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The outcome of a valid cognitive act. In Dharmakīrti's analysis, phala is not a product separate from the cognitive act; it is the act in its resultant aspect (pramiti). Knowing is its own result. No additional act is required to produce the knowing-that-one-has-known. This identification of pramāṇa with its phala has major consequences for the regress problem (see T6.01).
- **Quantum Measurement:** Measurement outcome. The registered eigenvalue after measurement is the closest table-supported analogue. The published QM concept table does not add an external interpretive framework beyond measurement outcome, state update, and quantum Bayesian update, so this section stays at that table-supported level.
- **Correspondence type:** Moderate parallel. Both resist reifying the result as an entity separate from the act. Full identification (pramāṇa = phala) has no direct QM equivalent; see BIAN-16.

---

## T2.04 — Vyavasāya (Determinate judgment / Epistemic verdict)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The cognitive act of determining that x is the case: the moment of propositional crystallization at which a cognition becomes an actionable epistemic commitment. Vyavasāya follows pratyakṣa and involves conceptual structure but is not reducible to mere perception. It is the point at which cognition becomes judgment.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-5 — Measurement Commitment. The moment at which an observer commits to a particular outcome as the result of a measurement. QM describes the physical process of state reduction; it has no formal account of the epistemic act by which an observer determines that a specific outcome is the case. Physical registration and epistemic commitment are collapsed into a single undefined event.
- **Structural gap:** QM merges the physical registration event with the epistemic commitment act. Buddhist Epistemology maintains the distinction formally.

---

## T2.05 — Svasaṃvedana as structural principle (Self-certification of cognition)

**Node:** N_BE_00011 — Svasaṃvedana (Skt.). Category: Type of perception.
**Related edges:** ED_BE_00019, ED_BE_00020 (see T1.06)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Beyond being a pramāṇa type (T1.06), svasaṃvedana functions as a universal structural principle: every valid cognition self-certifies its own occurrence. No regress of meta-cognitions is required to establish that a cognition occurred. The act certifies itself. This is not a separate second-order act; it is a structural feature of every first-order valid cognition.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-6 — Self-Certifying Measurement. Standard QM has no principle by which a measurement self-certifies its own occurrence. In the formalism, measurement is introduced through a measurement model or state-update rule rather than through an epistemic self-certification act. In practice, an experimenter determines that measurement has occurred, but this determination is not itself formalized as a self-certifying epistemic principle. Buddhist Epistemology eliminates this meta-level requirement via svasaṃvedana; the published QM table has no direct equivalent.
- **Structural gap:** Standard QM lacks a table-supported epistemic self-certification principle for measurement completion. Buddhist Epistemology does not require such an external meta-level in the same way. This is structurally related to the von Neumann chain problem.

---

## T2.06 — Kalpanā (Conceptual construction / Propositional overlay)

**Node:** N_BE_00008 — Vikalpa / Kalpanā (Skt.). Category: Mental structure.
**Definition:** Conceptualized, categorizing cognition that applies names and classes — contrasted with bare perception. The mind superimposes structures like proper names (yadṛcchā-śabda), genus-words (jāti-śabda), quality-words (guṇa-śabda), action-words (kriyā-śabda), and substance-words (dravya-śabda).

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00015: N_BE_00009 (Nirvikalpaka pratyakṣa) → N_BE_00008 — contrasted with (Oppositional)
- ED_BE_00038: N_BE_00002 (Pratyakṣa) → N_BE_00008 — is free from (Exclusionary)

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00013: N_BE_00008 → N_BE_00014 (Sāmānyalakṣaṇa) — operates on (Epistemic)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The cognitive act of imposing conceptual, linguistic, and relational structure onto raw perceptual content. Kalpanā is what transforms nirvikalpaka pratyakṣa into savikalpaka pratyakṣa. It is epistemically productive (enabling communication, inference, and action-planning) but also the source of most epistemic errors (bhrānti, saṃśaya, vikalpa).
- **Quantum Measurement:** Theoretical interpretation of measurement results. The act of embedding a raw measurement result into a physical theory: assigning it a variable name, a unit, a place in an equation, a physical interpretation. This is standard scientific practice but is not formalized within QM itself.
- **Correspondence type:** Functional parallel. Both describe the transition from raw perceptual-physical event to structured, communicable, theoretically meaningful content.

---

## T2.07 — Nirvikalpaka pratyakṣa (Non-conceptual perception / Pre-propositional apprehension)

**Node:** N_BE_00009 — Nirvikalpaka pratyakṣa (Skt.). Category: Mental structure.
**Definition:** Pure, direct perception free from conceptual or linguistic elaboration (nirvikalpaka, avyapadeśya). The object is the extensionless particular moment that disappears as soon as it appears.

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00014: N_BE_00009 → N_BE_00013 (Svalakṣaṇa) — apprehends (Epistemic)
- ED_BE_00015: N_BE_00009 → N_BE_00008 (Vikalpa) — contrasted with (Oppositional)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Perception entirely prior to kalpanā. Apprehends the svalakṣaṇa directly without any linguistic, categorical, or inferential processing. Linguistically ineffable; it cannot be stated without becoming savikalpaka. Epistemically foundational: it is the ground from which all valid cognition ultimately derives its contact with reality.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-7 — Pre-Conceptual Cognitive Stratum. QM can model the physical pre-readout interaction through system–meter coupling, von Neumann measurement, and generalized measurement. What it does not model is a non-conceptual cognitive apprehension analogous to nirvikalpaka pratyakṣa. The physical event can be represented formally; the pre-conceptual epistemic stratum cannot.
- **Structural gap:** The gap is not a missing physical interaction model. The gap is that QM has no formal category for non-conceptual cognition prior to symbolic or numerical interpretation.

---

## T2.08 — Savikalpaka pratyakṣa (Conceptual perception / Interpreted apprehension)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT. Implicit as the complement of N_BE_00009.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Perception already processed through kalpanā. Carries propositional structure: this is a pot, that is blue. Valid as a secondary cognitive form; not the epistemological foundation. It is what ordinary perception presents and what enables communication and inference.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Interpreted measurement result. The eigenvalue after it has been assigned physical meaning: this is 0.5 eV, that particle is spin-up. The standard output of any real experiment, as it appears in a lab notebook or published paper.
- **Correspondence type:** Functional parallel. Both describe the processed, usable, communicable form of a perceptual event.

# TIER 3 — Theory of Objects (Ontology of the Epistemic Object)

## T3.01 — Svalakṣaṇa (Particular / Unique causally efficacious instance)

**Node:** N_BE_00013 — Svalakṣaṇa (Skt.). Category: Ontology–cognition.
**Definition:** The individual, unique characteristic of a thing; the object of direct perception. Per Dharmakīrti: (a) has power to produce effects (arthakriyākāritva), (b) is specific (asādhāraṇa), (c) is not denotable by a word (śabdasyāviṣaya), (d) is apprehensible without depending upon other factors.

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00005: N_BE_00002 (Pratyakṣa) → N_BE_00013 — apprehends (Epistemic)
- ED_BE_00014: N_BE_00009 (Nirvikalpaka pratyakṣa) → N_BE_00013 — apprehends (Epistemic)
- ED_BE_00035: N_BE_00029 (Kṣaṇabhaṅgavāda) → N_BE_00013 — characterizes (Ontological)

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00032: N_BE_00013 → N_BE_00022 (Arthakriyā) — possesses (Ontological)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The real object. Momentary, unique, causally efficacious (arthakriyāśakti), and non-repeatable. It is what pratyakṣa actually contacts at the moment of valid perception. Its causal efficacy — its capacity to produce effects — is the sole criterion of its reality. It has no extension beyond its own moment.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Particular registered outcome / single measurement event. The closest QM analogue is not the eigenstate as a mathematical vector, but the definite registered outcome produced in one measurement context. The post-measurement eigenstate may formally represent the updated system state, but it is not identical to the Buddhist unique particular.
- **Correspondence type:** Moderate structural parallel. Both mark a concrete, event-level particular rather than a general class. Divergence: svalakṣaṇa is an epistemic-ontological particular; the QM outcome is a registered event within a measurement formalism.

---

## T3.02 — Sāmānyalakṣaṇa (Universal / Conceptual construct without direct causal power)

**Node:** N_BE_00014 — Sāmānyalakṣaṇa (Skt.). Category: Ontology–cognition.
**Definition:** A shared, general characteristic; the object of inference and conceptual thought. Per Dharmakīrti: (a) has no power to produce effects, (b) is common to many things, (c) is denotable by a word, (d) is not apprehensible without depending upon other factors such as verbal conventions.

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00006: N_BE_00003 (Anumāna) → N_BE_00014 — apprehends (Epistemic)
- ED_BE_00013: N_BE_00008 (Vikalpa) → N_BE_00014 — operates on (Epistemic)

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00033: N_BE_00014 → N_BE_00022 (Arthakriyā) — lacks (Ontological)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The conceptual universal. A mental construction abstracted across multiple svalakṣaṇa instances. Not causally efficacious on its own; real only as a cognitive object. It is the object of anumāna, not pratyakṣa. Dharmakīrti's apoha theory explains how sāmānyalakṣaṇa refer through exclusion rather than positive essence.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Superposition / wave function ψ. A linear combination of eigenstates: a formal predictive structure that represents possible outcomes rather than one registered actuality. It is not directly observed as an outcome, but it has predictive and physical significance through interference, relative phase, unitary evolution, and the Born rule.
- **Correspondence type:** Moderate structural parallel. Both organize possible or generalizable content rather than a single concrete particular. Divergence: sāmānyalakṣaṇa is a conceptual universal lacking causal efficacy in Buddhist epistemology, whereas ψ is an active formal state structure within QM prediction and dynamics.

---

## T3.03 — Arthakriyāśakti (Causal efficacy / Power to perform a function)

**Node:** N_BE_00022 — Arthakriyā (Skt.). Category: Ontology–cognition.
**Definition:** Double meaning: (1) Ontological: causal efficacy as the criterion of reality — "That which is able to perform a function exists ultimately." Only objects able to participate causally in producing phenomena are real. (2) Epistemological: to fulfill a practical purpose — valid cognitions provide the cognitive basis for successful human activities (puruṣārthasiddhi).

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00012: N_BE_00021 (Svabhāvapratibandha) → N_BE_00022 — has as basis (Ontological)
- ED_BE_00032: N_BE_00013 (Svalakṣaṇa) → N_BE_00022 — possesses (Ontological)
- ED_BE_00033: N_BE_00014 (Sāmānyalakṣaṇa) → N_BE_00022 — lacks (Ontological)
- ED_BE_00034: N_BE_00029 (Kṣaṇabhaṅgavāda) → N_BE_00022 — supports (Ontological)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The criterion of reality for Dharmakīrti. What is real is what can causally produce effects. Svalakṣaṇa has arthakriyāśakti; sāmānyalakṣaṇa does not. This criterion cleanly separates the real from the merely conceptual.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Operational distinguishability / empirical accessibility. A quantity has physical significance in QM when it can make a measurable or predictive difference. Global phase has no direct measurable status, while relative phase remains physically significant. The QM-side analogue is therefore functional and operational, not a claim that QM defines reality as "detectability."
- **Correspondence type:** Strong structural parallel. Both systems use causal-functional efficacy as their primary criterion of reality. Neither grounds reality in a mind-independent substance with intrinsic properties.

---

## T3.04 — Kṣaṇikatva / Kṣaṇikavāda (Momentariness / Doctrine of momentary existence)

**Node:** N_BE_00029 — Kṣaṇabhaṅgavāda (Skt.). Category: Ontology–cognition.
**Definition:** The Sautrāntika doctrine: "a moment disappears as soon as it appears without duration." Here it is used only as an epistemic-ontological account of momentary particulars in Pramāṇavāda. It can be structurally related to dependent arising, but the present mapping does not develop a broader metaphysical doctrine of temporal reality.

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00034: N_BE_00029 → N_BE_00022 (Arthakriyā) — supports (Ontological)
- ED_BE_00035: N_BE_00029 → N_BE_00013 (Svalakṣaṇa) — characterizes (Ontological)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** All real entities (svalakṣaṇa) exist for only one moment. Apparent persistence is a conceptual overlay (kalpanā) applied to a causal series of distinct momentary particulars. No entity endures through time; what appears to persist is a sequence, not a substance.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-8 — Quantum Temporal Discontinuity. QM does not have a doctrine of momentary existence. Time in QM is a continuous parameter in the Schrödinger equation. However, the measurement event introduces a discontinuity: the pre-measurement state and the post-measurement state are categorically different. Between measurements, evolution is continuous; at measurement, it is discontinuous. QM has the formal structure corresponding to kṣaṇikavāda but has not theorized it epistemologically. Decoherence theory describes the process mechanism; it does not address the epistemological question of what kind of entity exists between measurements.
- **Structural gap (partial):** QM instantiates the relevant discontinuity formally but does not possess an epistemological doctrine of temporal discreteness equivalent to kṣaṇikavāda.

---

## T3.05 — Svabhāva (Intrinsic nature — what Pramāṇavāda denies)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT. Implicit across ontology–cognition nodes.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Svabhāva is the concept of an entity having a fixed, intrinsic, context-independent nature. This mapping treats svabhāva-denial structurally, without asserting doctrinal identity between Pramāṇavāda and Madhyamaka. In the present epistemological frame, the nature of a particular is analyzed through relational and causal conditions rather than as a context-independent essence.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Context-independent hidden-variable state. The relevant assumption is that a quantum system has definite, pre-existing values independently of the measurement context. Bell's theorem and hidden-variable constraints in the published QM concept table support a negative structural parallel: measurement outcomes cannot be treated as simply revealing pre-existing local definite properties.
- **Correspondence type:** Negative structural parallel. Both systems reject context-independent intrinsic properties as a legitimate explanatory basis, but the parallel is methodological and structural rather than historical, doctrinal, or ontological.

---

## T3.06 — Abhāva (Absence / Non-entity as epistemic object)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Absence is a real epistemic object. Knowing that the pot is not on the table is a valid cognitive act (anupalabdhi). Abhāva has its own ontological status as the object of absence-cognition. Some schools (Kumārila Bhaṭṭa) treat anupalabdhi as a distinct pramāṇa; Dharmakīrti integrates it into the pratyakṣa-anumāna framework.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-9 — Formal Absence Cognition. QM formally includes no-result / null measurement: the absence of a detector click can be a measurement outcome that carries information and updates the quantum state. This partially overlaps with absence-cognition. However, QM treats the no-click event as a physical measurement outcome and state update, not as a distinct epistemic category like abhāva/anupalabdhi.
- **Structural gap:** The gap is not that QM lacks no-result outcomes; the gap is that QM does not theorize absence as a distinct epistemic category of absence-cognition.

---

## T3.07 — Sāmānya (Generic property / Repeatable attribute)

**Node:** N_BE_00014 — Sāmānyalakṣaṇa (Skt.). Same node as T3.02; different aspect.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Repeatable, shareable attributes that multiple svalakṣaṇa can instantiate. Real as cognitive objects and instrumentally necessary for inference and communication. Not real as external universals in the Nyāya sense; real only as epistemically functional constructs.
- **Quantum Measurement:** [EXTRAPOLATED] Repeatable physical properties such as spin may provide a loose structural analogy to generic repeatability, but quantum numbers and conserved quantities as a class are not formal categories in the published QM measurement table.
- **Correspondence type:** Weak structural analogy. Both involve repeatable, shareable properties, but the QM-side example is extrapolated beyond the published measurement table and should not be treated as a core table-supported correspondence.

---

## T3.08 — Tādātmya and Tadutpatti as object-relations (Identity and causation between objects)

**Node:** N_BE_00021 — Svabhāvapratibandha (Skt.). Category: Logic.
**Definition:** Dharmakīrti's innovation providing universal foundation for inference. Two types: tadutpatti (causality — relation between effect and cause) and tādātmya (identity — relation between two essentially common individuals, e.g., vṛkṣa/tree and siṃśapā/oak). Replaces Dignāga's purely inductive vyāpti with a deductive, ontology-grounded relation.

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00011: N_BE_00019 (Vyāpti) → N_BE_00021 — grounded in (Logical)
- ED_BE_00012: N_BE_00021 → N_BE_00022 (Arthakriyā) — has as basis (Ontological)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The two valid inter-object relations that can ground inference. The following account is a structural simplification; classical examples and formulations vary across Buddhist logic discussions. Tādātmya indicates an identity or co-nature relation; tadutpatti indicates causal production. Both function as relations that can ground valid anumāna rather than as merely conceptual associations.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Entanglement correlations. Quantum entanglement produces invariant correlations between measurement outcomes on spatially separated systems. These correlations are not causal in the local sense (no signaling possible; Bell inequality violated) and not relations of shared intrinsic nature (svabhāva is denied). In VVV-QMRF, entanglement is treated as an extension relation category rather than a third classical subtype of Dharmakīrti's *svabhāvapratibandha* taxonomy.
- **Correspondence type:** Partial. Entanglement correlations do not map cleanly onto either tādātmya or tadutpatti.
- **BIAN-10 — Non-Classical Correlation Relation.** Quantum entanglement constitutes a non-causal, non-identity correlation structure in QM. Buddhist Epistemology has no classical *svabhāvapratibandha* subtype for this structure beyond *tādātmya* and *tadutpatti*, so VVV-QMRF treats IRB as an extension relation category. (Note: this BIAN runs in the opposite direction — Buddhist Epistemology lacks a category-language slot for a QM structure.)

# TIER 4 — Error Theory (Epistemology of False Cognition)

## T4.01 — Bhrānti (Erroneous cognition / Illusion)

**Node:** N_BE_00006 — Bhrānti (Skt.). Category: Epistemic error.
**Definition:** A mistaken apprehension of reality; an inversion of the true nature of an object. In the conventional world, mistaking a mirage for water is mithyā-saṃvṛti (erroneous conventional truth).

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00027: N_BE_00023 (Avidyā) → N_BE_00006 — causes (Causal)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Cognition that presents its object in a manner that does not correspond to the object's causal structure. The classic examples: perceiving a rope as a snake; perceiving a distant moving object as stationary. Bhrānti is a real cognition (it occurs, it has ākāra, it produces action) but epistemically invalid (it is not avisaṃvādin). Bhrānti is to be formally distinguished from saṃśaya (doubt), anadhyavasāya (non-determination), and vikalpa (conceptual fiction).
- **Quantum Measurement:** Measurement error / systematic bias. A measurement that returns an incorrect value due to apparatus malfunction, calibration error, selection bias, or systematic confound. A real physical process; an epistemically invalid result. The event occurs and produces a registered number; the number does not correctly represent the target observable.
- **Correspondence type:** Functional parallel. Both are real cognitive-physical events producing epistemically invalid output.

---

## T4.02 — Saṃśaya (Doubt / Indeterminate cognition)

**Node:** N_BE_00007 — Saṃśaya (Skt.). Category: Epistemic error.
**Definition:** [uncertain] A state of indecision in which neither alternative is definitively established. The pursuit of certainty (nirṇaya) requires some initial doubt (saṃśaya) or desire to know (jijñāsā) as its motivation.

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00028: N_BE_00023 (Avidyā) → N_BE_00007 — gives rise to (Causal) [Note: uncertain]

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00037: N_BE_00007 → N_BE_00027 (Svārthānumāna) — motivates (Psychological) [Note: uncertain]

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Cognition that oscillates between two or more incompatible determinations without resolving. Active uncertainty: the cognitive agent holds x and non-x as simultaneously possible without committing to either. Not a valid pramāṇa. Requires resolution via pratyakṣa or anumāna.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Pre-measurement epistemic state. An observer who knows the wave function but not the outcome is in a state of genuine indeterminacy about which eigenvalue will result. This is partially modeled as physical superposition, but the epistemic state of the observer holding this description is not formally theorized.
- **Correspondence type:** Partial.
- **BIAN-11 — Observer Epistemic Indeterminacy.** The formal epistemic state of an observer who knows ψ but not the outcome. QM describes the physical superposition. It does not describe the epistemic state of the agent who holds the description. The agent's pre-measurement epistemic condition — the analogue of saṃśaya — is outside the formalism.

## T4.03 — Tarka (Counterfactual reasoning / Reductio reasoning)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** A form of reasoning that establishes a thesis by demonstrating the absurdity of its negation. Not an independent pramāṇa but a supporting tool for anumāna. Used specifically to establish vyāpti (invariant pervasion) by showing what would follow if the pervasion did not hold.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Bell-type no-go reasoning and hidden-variable constraints, as listed in the published QM concept table. They establish constraints on quantum reality by showing what follows if assumptions such as local realism or pre-existing hidden variables are imposed. The reasoning is reductive: the assumption conflicts with quantum predictions and experimental violations of Bell-type inequalities.
- **Correspondence type:** Strong structural parallel. Both use reductio methodology to establish foundational constraints on what kinds of entities and relations are possible within their respective frameworks.

---

## T4.04 — Bādhaka pramāṇa (Invalidating cognition / Epistemic override)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** A subsequent valid cognition that formally overrides and invalidates a prior cognition. The earlier cognition is revealed as bhrānti. This is a formal mechanism of epistemic correction built into the system. Dharmakīrti distinguishes conditions under which bādhaka pramāṇa can operate and its relationship to svataḥ and parataḥ prāmāṇya (see T6.03).
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-12 — Formal Measurement Invalidation. QM has no formal mechanism by which a subsequent measurement invalidates the epistemic status of a prior measurement. A new measurement updates the quantum state; it does not retroactively address whether the prior result was epistemically valid or invalid. Quantum error correction codes correct physical errors in quantum states; they do not constitute an epistemological theory of measurement invalidation.
- **Structural gap:** QM lacks a formal epistemological mechanism for measurement revision and correction at the level of epistemic status. This gap propagates into the absence of a formal account of how experimental anomalies resolve prior measurement claims.

---

## T4.05 — Anadhyavasāya (Non-determination / Failure to cognize)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The epistemic state in which an object is causally present and capable of producing cognition, but no determination occurs. Neither valid cognition nor erroneous cognition — no cognition at all. Distinct from saṃśaya (which involves active oscillation) and from bhrānti (which involves misdetermined cognition).
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-13 — Null Observer Event. The state of a measurement apparatus for which a quantum event occurs but produces no registration — a detector that fails to fire when a particle passes through it. The closest table-supported analogue is no-result / null measurement when the absence of a click itself carries information and updates the quantum state. QM does not theorize the epistemic state of an observer for whom an event is causally present but produces no cognition.
- **Structural gap:** QM has table-supported no-result outcomes, but it does not have an epistemological category for non-engagement as a distinct cognitive state.

---

## T4.06 — Smṛti (Memory / Retained cognition)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Cognition that takes a previously experienced object as its content. Not a valid pramāṇa (it does not produce new contact with the object) but epistemically relevant as the basis for comparative inference and as a source of error when confused with fresh pratyakṣa.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Classical measurement record. The stored output of a prior measurement. Not a new measurement but retained data. Plays a role in sequential measurement records and statistical updating of state estimates.
- **Correspondence type:** Functional parallel. Both are retained, non-fresh epistemic content. Both are epistemically secondary. Both can become sources of error when treated as fresh evidence.

---

## T4.07 — Vikalpa (Conceptual fiction / Purely conceptual construct with no corresponding particular)

**Node:** N_BE_00008 — Vikalpa / Kalpanā (Skt.). Same node as T2.06; different aspect.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** A concept that has no corresponding svalakṣaṇa as causal ground. It refers only within the conceptual domain (sāmānyalakṣaṇa) without grounding in a real particular. Examples: a rabbit's horn; a permanent self; a universal substance. Epistemically empty in terms of arthakriyāśakti.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Unmeasurable theoretical construct. A formal quantity that appears in the mathematical framework but produces no observable prediction. Example: the global phase of a wave function, since only relative phases have measurable significance.
- **Correspondence type:** Structural parallel. Both are formally well-defined entities that fail the causal-efficacy criterion of reality.

# TIER 5 — Theory of Inference (Anumāna Architecture)

## T5.01 — Liṅga (Logical mark / Inferential sign)

**Node:** N_BE_00016 — Liṅga / Hetu (Skt.). Category: Logic.
**Definition:** The inferential mark or logical reason used to derive a conclusion (e.g., smoke → fire). A property (dharma) located in the property-possessor (dharmin, pakṣa) that serves as evidence for inferring the probandum (sādhyadharma, liṅgin).

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00007: N_BE_00003 (Anumāna) → N_BE_00016 — operates through (Logical)

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00009: N_BE_00016 → N_BE_00017 (Pakṣa) — qualifies (Logical)
- ED_BE_00010: N_BE_00016 → N_BE_00019 (Vyāpti) — requires (Logical)
- ED_BE_00039: N_BE_00016 → N_BE_00020 (Avinābhāva) — requires (Logical)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The evidence term in an inference. That from which the sādhya (probandum) is inferred. Must satisfy three conditions (trairūpya: see T5.06) to ground valid anumāna. Liṅga is the epistemically accessible fact from which the inaccessible fact is derived.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Measurement pointer state. The macroscopic, classically readable state of a measurement apparatus after interaction with the quantum system. The accessible evidence from which the quantum state is inferred: the meter reading, the detector click, the track in a bubble chamber.
- **Correspondence type:** Strong structural parallel. Both are the accessible indicator of an inaccessible underlying fact.

---

## T5.02 — Sādhya (Probandum / What is to be proved / Inferred object)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT. Implicit in the liṅga-sādhya relation (N_BE_00016 definition).

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The fact to be established by inference. Typically beyond direct perceptual reach. Liṅga is the accessible evidence; sādhya is the inferred conclusion.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Pre-measurement quantum state / wave function. What is inferred from the pattern of measurement outcomes. Not directly observable; inferred from repeated measurement-outcome statistics.
- **Correspondence type:** Structural parallel.

---

## T5.03 — Pakṣa (Logical subject / The case under consideration)

**Node:** N_BE_00017 — Pakṣa (Skt.). Category: Logic.
**Definition:** The proposition to be established in a Buddhist syllogism; also the argument's subject (dharmin), the locus where both the logical reason (hetu) and the property to be established (sādhya) co-occur.

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00009: N_BE_00016 (Liṅga) → N_BE_00017 — qualifies (Logical)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The subject of the inference. The entity about which the inferential claim is made. The hill, in the classic inference: the hill has fire because it has smoke.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Quantum system under measurement. The specific physical system about which the measurement statement is made: this electron, this photon, this ensemble of atoms.
- **Correspondence type:** Direct parallel.

---

## T5.04 — Sapakṣa (Positive confirming case / Similar instance)

**Node:** Sub-component of N_BE_00018 (Trairūpya) — the second condition.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Cases known to have both liṅga and sādhya. Establishes the positive correlation required by vyāpti. The kitchen is sapakṣa for the smoke-fire inference: both smoke and fire are present.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Confirming experimental runs. Measurement trials in which the predicted correlation between preparation and outcome is observed. Establishes the empirical pattern that grounds the Born rule application in a given context.
- **Correspondence type:** Functional parallel.

---

## T5.05 — Vipakṣa (Negative excluding case / Dissimilar instance)

**Node:** Sub-component of N_BE_00018 (Trairūpya) — the third condition.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Cases known to lack the sādhya. Establishes that liṅga is absent wherever sādhya is absent. The lake is vipakṣa for the smoke-fire inference: no fire, and indeed no smoke.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Disconfirming experimental runs / null results. Measurement trials in which the predicted outcome does not occur. Establishes the boundary conditions and failure modes of a quantum measurement protocol.
- **Correspondence type:** Functional parallel.

---

## T5.06 — Trairūpya (Three conditions of a valid inferential mark)

**Node:** N_BE_00018 — Trairūpya (Skt.). Category: Logic.
**Definition:** The three conditions for a valid reason (trirūpa-hetu): (i) pakṣadharmatva — the reason must be a property of the subject; (ii) anvaya / sapakṣe sattvam — presence in similar cases (positive concomitance); (iii) vyatireka / vipakṣe 'sattvam — absence in dissimilar cases (negative concomitance).

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00008: N_BE_00003 (Anumāna) → N_BE_00018 — validated by (Logical)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The three necessary and sufficient conditions for a liṅga to ground valid anumāna. (1) Pakṣa-dharmatā: the liṅga is present in the pakṣa. (2) Sapakṣa-sattvam: the liṅga co-occurs with sādhya in at least some similar cases. (3) Vipakṣa-asattvam: the liṅga is entirely absent from all cases in which sādhya is absent. All three must hold; any failure generates a hetvābhāsa (see T5.10 through T5.13).
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-14 — Tripartite Measurement Validity Conditions. QM has no formally unified three-condition structure for when measurement data constitutes valid inferential evidence for a theoretical claim. Repeated measurement-outcome statistics and Bell inequality violations serve partial evidential functions. But these are not integrated into a unified formal structure equivalent to trairūpya. Experimental physics has methodological norms; it does not have a formal epistemological theory of when a measurement constitutes valid evidence.
- **Structural gap:** QM lacks a formal unified account of valid evidential inference from measurement data.

---

## T5.07 — Vyāpti (Invariant pervasion / Necessary concomitance)

**Node:** N_BE_00019 — Vyāpti (Skt.). Category: Logic.
**Definition:** The logical relation of pervasion between probans (hetu) and probandum (sādhya) that guarantees successful inference. Dignāga introduced this concept but did not explain how the relation could be universalized — a gap later addressed by Dharmakīrti's svabhāvapratibandha.

**Inbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00010: N_BE_00016 (Liṅga) → N_BE_00019 — requires (Logical)
- ED_BE_00026: N_BE_00020 (Avinābhāva) → N_BE_00019 — is precursor of (Logical)

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00011: N_BE_00019 → N_BE_00021 (Svabhāvapratibandha) — grounded in (Logical)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The invariant relation between liṅga and sādhya that grounds all anumāna. Wherever liṅga, there sādhya; wherever not-sādhya, there not-liṅga. Not merely a statistical correlation but a nomologically necessary relation within the domain. Establishing vyāpti is the central epistemological problem of inference theory. It cannot be established by any finite enumeration of cases (a point Dharmakīrti addresses through tarka and the analysis of tādātmya / tadutpatti).
- **Quantum Measurement:** Born rule. The invariant probabilistic bridge from amplitude to outcome probability: P(outcome) = |⟨eigenstate|ψ⟩|². It is necessary within standard QM prediction and empirically confirmed, but it yields probabilities rather than deterministic concomitance.
- **Correspondence type:** Functional analogy with major divergence. Both are bridge rules that make systematic prediction possible. Divergence: vyāpti is an invariant pervasion relation for valid inference, whereas the Born rule is a probabilistic mapping from formal state structure to measurement statistics.

---

## T5.08 — Kevalānvayin (Mark present in all cases / Universal positive mark)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** A liṅga that is present in every possible case, leaving no vipakṣa. Because everything that exists is, for example, knowable, knowability cannot be established as a liṅga by reference to vipakṣa. Special treatment is required: the inference must be established by pakṣa-dharmatā and sapakṣa-sattvam alone.
- **Quantum Measurement:** [EXTRAPOLATED] Conserved observables may provide a loose structural analogy, but this is not a formal category in the published QM measurement table.
- **Correspondence type:** Structural parallel at the level of universality.

---

## T5.09 — Kevalavyatirekin (Mark present only in negative cases / Purely contrastive mark)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** A liṅga established entirely by the contrapositive: whenever sādhya is absent, liṅga is absent. No positive sapakṣa is available. The inference is grounded purely in contrastive exclusion.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-15 — Purely Contrastive Quantum Evidence. No formal QM category for evidence established entirely through null results and contrapositive structure. The closest table-supported analogue is no-result / null measurement, where the absence of a detector click still carries information and updates the quantum state.
- **Structural gap:** QM has no formal purely-contrastive evidential category.

---

## T5.10 — Hetvābhāsa (Pseudo-mark / Fallacious inferential sign)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** A liṅga that appears to satisfy trairūpya but fails on examination. Generates invalid inference. Dharmakīrti identifies three primary subtypes: asiddha (unestablished), viruddha (contrary), anāikāntika (non-exclusive). Each produces a characteristic type of epistemic failure.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Measurement artifact / systematic confound. A measurement result that appears to confirm a hypothesis but arises from systematic error, apparatus coupling, selection bias, or confounding interaction. Each subtype of hetvābhāsa has a functional analogue in experimental design.
- **Correspondence type:** Functional parallel. Divergence: Dharmakīrti has a formal taxonomy of fallacy types with defined subtypes and diagnostic conditions. Experimental physics has methodological heuristics for artifacts but no unified formal taxonomy.

---

## T5.11 — Asiddha hetvābhāsa (Unestablished mark / Liṅga not present in pakṣa)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The liṅga is not actually present in the subject of inference. The evidential basis is simply absent. The inference proceeds from a non-existent ground.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Null detector / failed apparatus. The measurement apparatus did not actually interact with the system. No valid inference can be drawn from the null output.
- **Correspondence type:** Direct functional parallel.

---

## T5.12 — Viruddha hetvābhāsa (Contrary mark / Liṅga that proves the opposite)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The liṅga actually establishes the negation of the intended sādhya. The evidence, properly analyzed, proves the opposite conclusion.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Inverted result / sign error. A measurement result that, due to calibration inversion or sign convention error, systematically indicates the opposite of the correct outcome.
- **Correspondence type:** Direct functional parallel.

---

## T5.13 — Anāikāntika hetvābhāsa (Non-exclusive mark / Liṅga present in both sapakṣa and vipakṣa)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The liṅga appears in cases with and without the sādhya. The correlation is imperfect; the liṅga is present in both confirming and disconfirming instances and therefore cannot ground a valid inference.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Noisy, low-fidelity, or weakly correlated measurement. A measurement whose readout is insufficiently correlated with the target quantum property, so the same pointer indication can appear in both confirming and disconfirming contexts. This is distinct from unselective measurement, where outcomes are ignored or averaged over.
- **Correspondence type:** Direct functional parallel, once the QM analogue is restricted to low-fidelity or weakly correlated evidence rather than unselective measurement.

---

## T5.14 — Tādātmya as inference ground (Identity-of-nature relation)

**Node:** N_BE_00021 — Svabhāvapratibandha (See T3.08). Category: Logic.
**Related edges:** ED_BE_00011, ED_BE_00012 (see T3.08)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The first of the two valid grounds for anumāna. A relation of essential co-nature between liṅga and sādhya: the two share an essential production relation or identity of nature such that the presence of one invariably accompanies the presence of the other. Example: the inference from smoke to fire is grounded in their shared nature of production from a common source.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Correlated quantum properties from common preparation. When two observable properties of a quantum system co-occur because they are both determined by the same preparation procedure or conservation law, their correlation is grounded in a shared production relation. Example: conservation of angular momentum in pair production correlates spin outcomes across detectors.
- **Correspondence type:** Partial. Conservation-based correlations approximate tadutpatti; any comparison with tādātmya is only an analogy to invariance, not Tādātmya proper. Entanglement correlations exceed both tādātmya and tadutpatti (see BIAN-10).

---

## T5.15 — Tadutpatti as inference ground (Causal production relation)

**Node:** N_BE_00021 — Svabhāvapratibandha (See T3.08). Category: Logic.
**Related edges:** ED_BE_00011, ED_BE_00012 (see T3.08)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The second of the two valid grounds for anumāna. A relation of causal production between liṅga and sādhya: the presence of liṅga is caused by the presence of sādhya. Example: smoke is caused by fire; the presence of smoke is thus a reliable indicator of fire.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Schrödinger evolution. The deterministic, unitary time-evolution of the quantum state between measurements, governed by the Hamiltonian. Causal, continuous, fully determined. Given the state at t₁, the state at t₂ is determined by the Schrödinger equation.
- **Correspondence type:** Partial. Both are deterministic causal relations. Critical asymmetry: Schrödinger evolution is suspended at the moment of measurement. Tadutpatti has no analogous suspension. The causal determinism of tadutpatti is universal; the causal determinism of Schrödinger evolution is interrupted by every measurement event.

# TIER 6 — Meta-Epistemology (Epistemology of Epistemology)

## T6.01 — Pramāṇa-phala identity (Cognition is its own result)

**Node:** N_BE_00001 (Pramāṇa) — specifically the phala component (pramāṇaphala).
**Related edge:** ED_BE_00004: N_BE_00001 → pramāṇaphala — produces (Structural)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Dharmakīrti's position: the result (phala) of a cognitive act is not a product separate from the act. Knowing is the knowing-of-the-result. No additional act is required to register that a cognition has occurred and produced a result. The act is self-completing: pramāṇa and phala are identified. This eliminates a potential regress: if phala were a separate product, one would need a further cognition to register the phala, and so on without end.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-16 — Measurement Self-Completion. The published QM table does not formally specify whether a measurement is self-completing or requires a subsequent act such as physical registration, recording, observer update, or environmental stabilization to be complete. From this mapping's perspective, this is a meta-epistemological pressure point in the measurement problem. Buddhist Epistemology resolves the analogous regress through pramāṇa-phala identity: the act certifies itself.
- **Structural gap:** This is the meta-epistemological core of the mapping. In this mapping, the absence of a direct QM equivalent to pramāṇa-phala identity is treated as a pressure point in the measurement problem, not as a claim that QM has a single formal source of failure.

## T6.02 — Svasaṃvedana as meta-epistemological principle (Self-certification without regress)

**Node:** N_BE_00011 — Svasaṃvedana (Skt.).
**Related edges:** ED_BE_00019, ED_BE_00020 (see T1.06, T2.05)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Valid cognition does not require a second-order cognition to certify it as having occurred. This stops the regress: you do not need to know-that-you-know in order to know. The first-order act certifies itself through svasaṃvedana. This is the foundational principle of the entire Pramāṇavāda system. It is not a claim about consciousness in the phenomenal sense; it is a formal claim about the structure of valid epistemic acts.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-17 — Regress-Stopping Principle. Standard QM describes system-apparatus coupling and the Heisenberg cut but does not provide an internal epistemic principle equivalent to svasaṃvedana. The von Neumann chain shows the regress pressure: the system interacts with an apparatus, the apparatus can itself be treated as a quantum system, and further registration can be requested again. Decoherence explains environmental suppression of coherences, but in the published QM table it does not by itself supply a self-certifying epistemic act.
- **Structural gap:** Deepest meta-epistemological asymmetry. From this mapping's perspective, the absence of an internal epistemic regress-stopping principle is what makes the von Neumann chain structurally salient. Svasaṃvedana is Pramāṇavāda's self-certification principle; the published QM table has no direct equivalent.

## T6.03 — Svataḥ prāmāṇya vs. Parataḥ prāmāṇya (Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic validity)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** A foundational meta-epistemological debate. Svataḥ prāmāṇya: a cognition's validity is established by the cognition itself (Mīmāṃsaka position, partially accommodated by Dharmakīrti). Parataḥ prāmāṇya: validity is established by a subsequent cognition. Dharmakīrti's complex position: initial validity is intrinsic (via svasaṃvedana and arthakriyāśakti); invalidity is detected extrinsically (via bādhaka pramāṇa).
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-18 — Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic Measurement Validity. QM does not formalize whether a measurement's validity is established by the measurement itself or by subsequent verification. In experimental practice, extrinsic verification is standard: cross-checks, calibration runs, repetition, statistical analysis. But none of this is integrated into the formalism. QM has no formal analogue to either the Mīmāṃsaka or the Dharmakīrti position on this question.
- **Structural gap:** QM has no formal account of where measurement validity is located.

## T6.04 — Anātmavāda as epistemological commitment (No-self as structural principle)

**Node:** No separate node in the BE SOT. Implicit in momentariness (N_BE_00029) and process ontology.

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** The denial of a permanent, unified self-entity (ātman) underlying cognition is not merely a metaphysical claim in Pramāṇavāda; it is a structural epistemological commitment. The pramātṛ is not a substance but a causal series of cognitive events (santāna). The knower is a process, not a fixed point. This directly shapes the epistemology: there is no persistent subject of cognition to whom all results are presented; there are only momentary cognitive events in causal series.
- **Quantum Measurement:** BIAN-19 — Observer as Process, not Substance. QM's observer is formally undefined: the apparatus may be treated as a quantum system in a larger Hilbert space or as a classical reference point across a Heisenberg cut. Standard QM does not require an ātman-like substantial observer, but it also does not formally analyze the observer as a causal series of cognitive events.
- **Structural gap:** The contrast is not a substantive-observer claim inside QM. The contrast is that standard formalism leaves the observer's process-structure unanalyzed, whereas Buddhist Epistemology explicitly theorizes the knower as process rather than substance.

## T6.05 — Apoha theory as meta-semantic principle (Meaning through exclusion, not essence)

**Node:** N_BE_00015 — Apoha / Anyāpoha (Skt.). Category: Philosophy of language.
**Definition:** Meaning is conveyed by excluding what something is not (e.g., not-non-cow → cow). Dignāga's theory: "a word expresses its own object through the exclusion of the other [things], just as an inferential mark establishes the object to be proved through exclusion." Verbal cognition is not an independent pramāṇa but a variety of inference.

**Outbound edges:**
- ED_BE_00016: N_BE_00015 → N_BE_00003 (Anumāna) — is linguistic variant of (Logical)

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** All conceptual meaning is constituted through exclusion (apoha): a term refers by excluding what it does not denote, not by picking out a positive essence. This applies at the meta-level: the term pramāṇa itself refers through exclusion of non-pramāṇa events. Positive essence is not a legitimate semantic category in the Pramāṇavāda framework.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Complementarity and mutual exclusion of observables. Bohr's complementarity: conjugate observables (position-momentum, energy-time) are mutually exclusive; the definition of one excludes the simultaneous definite determination of the other. Physical meaning in QM is partly constituted by exclusion relations between incompatible observables. An observable is defined partly by what it is not simultaneously compatible with.
- **Correspondence type:** Structural parallel at the meta-semantic level. Both ground meaning in exclusion relations rather than positive essences.

## T6.06 — Pramāṇa as action-guiding (Epistemology grounded in praxis)

**Node:** N_BE_00022 — Arthakriyā (Skt.) — epistemological aspect. Category: Ontology–cognition.
**Definition (epistemological aspect):** To fulfill a practical purpose — valid cognitions provide the cognitive basis for successful human activities (puruṣārthasiddhi).

- **Buddhist Epistemology:** Valid cognition is ultimately defined and validated by its capacity to guide successful action (arthakriyā): the capacity to produce the expected result when acted upon. A cognition that fails to guide action successfully is retroactively invalidated by bādhaka pramāṇa. Epistemology is not merely theoretical; it is grounded in pragmatic efficacy. This is not a concession to pragmatism from outside the system; it is the criterion built into the definition of pramāṇa.
- **Quantum Measurement:** Predictive success as the validity criterion of the theory. The broader quantum formalism is warranted by repeated predictive success across measurement contexts. The Born rule is accepted because it reliably guides successful experimental action. Within the present epistemological mapping, its warrant is treated as empirical-pragmatic rather than as a direct disclosure of intrinsic nature.
- **Correspondence type:** Deep structural parallel at the meta-level. Both systems ultimately ground epistemic validity in practical efficacy, not in correspondence to a mind-independent reality. Both are structurally pragmatist in their validity criteria. This is the deepest meta-level convergence in the mapping.
