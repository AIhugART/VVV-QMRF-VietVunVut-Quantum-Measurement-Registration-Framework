## Framework lens key

- A1 Dependent Arising: relational conditioning, causal dependence, system-meter coupling, process, correlation, and trajectory.
- A2 Emptiness: absence of intrinsic properties, critique of substance, self, realism, universals, or pre-existing determinate properties.
- A3 Consciousness-Only: cognition, perception, measurement, state assignment, manifestation through an epistemic relation, and state update.
- A4 Two Truths: conventional versus ultimate description, classical versus quantum tiering, conceptual construction, language, interpretation, and the Heisenberg cut.

## RCA rules

- Each row is 1:1 from one Buddhist Epistemology concept to one Quantum Measurement concept.
- Repeated Quantum Measurement targets are allowed because the Buddhist table has 232 rows and the Quantum Measurement table has 105 rows.
- Cross-domain links are structural analogies only, not doctrinal, physical, historical, or ontological equivalences.
- `Strong` means close formal or functional analogy.
- `Medium` means clear structural analogy without formal equivalence.
- `Weak` means nearest available 1:1 anchor with limited analogy, including rows with no direct Quantum Measurement analogue but no canonical BIAN assignment.
- `BIAN-n` is reserved for canonical BIAN labels defined in `documents/research_documents/mapping/Buddhist_Epistemology_and_Quantum_Measurement_system_mapping.md` L833-L856.
- BIAN labels identify structural Buddhist Epistemology concepts absent from Quantum Measurement formalism; they are not generic table-completion markers.
- Evidence columns cite the source-table line number and the source evidence already carried inside the source tables.
- Canonical QM terms in the `QM concept` column keep their original physical meaning from the QM source table; this codex does not rename or redefine them.
- When a row later supports VVV-EQM registration analysis, the QM column names the nearest physical anchor only; the VVV-EQM novelty belongs to the registration/validity layer, not to a modified QM law.
- Unless a row explicitly discusses human cognition, observer-language in structural reasons should be read at the level of a general registering system.
