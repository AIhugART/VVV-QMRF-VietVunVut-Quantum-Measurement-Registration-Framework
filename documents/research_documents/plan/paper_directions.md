## Direction A — Systematic Comparison of BEI with Relational QM and QBism

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| **Difficulty** | Medium |
| **Estimated Time** | 6–9 months |
| **Publication Risk** | Low (2/5) |

### Core Thesis

The axioms of the Buddhist Epistemic Interpretation (BEI) are structurally and logically equivalent to those of Rovelli's Relational Quantum Mechanics (RQM) and Fuchs' QBism — yet they originate from a non-Western philosophical tradition. The contribution is twofold: (1) demonstrating this is an *independent convergence*, not a derivative borrowing, and (2) thereby increasing the robustness and credibility of all three frameworks through cross-traditional triangulation.

### Method

Formal comparative analysis. Axiom-to-axiom mapping across BEI, RQM, and QBism. No new mathematics required — the paper operates at the epistemological and structural level.

### Research Gap

No systematic three-way comparison of RQM × QBism × Buddhist epistemology exists in a single paper. Each pair has been discussed in isolation (RQM–QBism, QBism–Buddhism) but the triangular mapping remains absent.

### Target Journals

- *Foundations of Physics*
- *Studies in History and Philosophy of Modern Physics*
- *European Journal for Philosophy of Science*

## Direction B — Resolving the Wigner's Friend Paradox via Madhyamaka Relational Ontology

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| **Difficulty** | High |
| **Estimated Time** | 9–14 months |
| **Publication Risk** | Medium (3/5) |

### Core Thesis

The Wigner's Friend paradox arises from the assumption that an *absolute observer* exists — a privileged reference frame from which facts are universally definite. Madhyamaka Śūnyatā (emptiness) systematically dismantles this assumption: no entity, including an observer, possesses *svabhāva* (intrinsic, independent existence). The paper proposes a formal resolution of the Wigner's Friend scenario grounded in relational ontology, showing that the paradox dissolves when the notion of an absolute, observer-independent fact is rejected.

### Method

Requires thorough command of: (1) the Proietti et al. (2019) extended Wigner's Friend experimental test, (2) the Deutsch (1985) no-go theorem for single-world interpretations. Construct a formal argument using relational logic or elementary category theory to demonstrate that WF contradictions require the premise of observer-independent facts — a premise Madhyamaka rejects on independent philosophical grounds.

### Research Gap

Existing resolutions (Many-Worlds, RQM, QBism, consistent histories) each face well-known objections. A formal philosophical resolution drawing on a systematic non-Western epistemological tradition is absent from the literature.

### Target Journals

- *Foundations of Physics*
- *Physical Review A* (Letter)
- *Synthese*

## Direction C — Ālayavijñāna as a Formal Model for Quantum State Space

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| **Difficulty** | Very High |
| **Estimated Time** | 12–18 months |
| **Publication Risk** | High (4/5) |

### Core Thesis

Construct a formal mathematical model in which Hilbert space is reinterpreted through the structure of Ālayavijñāna (storehouse consciousness) from Yogācāra Buddhism. The mapping is structural: (1) the Ālaya as the container of all potential states (bīja / seeds), (2) bīja as latent propensity amplitudes, (3) manifestation conditions (*pratyaya*) as the analog of measurement context or environmental decoherence triggering actualization. This is a genuinely new ontological proposal — not an interpretation grafted onto existing QM, but a re-foundation of what the state space *is*.

### Method

Demands significant mathematical sophistication. Likely tools: topos theory (Isham–Butterfield–Döring approach to quantum logic) or sheaf semantics to model "potentiality without actuality" — states that exist as *dispositional* rather than *occurrent*. Requires collaboration with a mathematician or logician specializing in categorical quantum mechanics.

### Research Gap

This is *terra incognita*. No one has attempted a formal mathematical model of quantum state space grounded in Buddhist epistemological categories. The risk of failure is high, but if successful, the contribution would be genuinely novel — a new ontological foundation for quantum theory from outside the Western analytic tradition.

### Target Journals

- *Journal of Mathematical Physics*
- *Foundations of Physics*
- *International Journal of Theoretical Physics*

## Direction D — BEI as a Meta-Framework for All Interpretations of Quantum Mechanics

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| **Difficulty** | Medium-High |
| **Estimated Time** | 8–12 months |
| **Publication Risk** | Medium (3/5) |

### Core Thesis

Rather than proposing BEI as *yet another interpretation* of quantum mechanics, this paper argues that BEI functions as an *epistemological meta-framework* under which multiple existing interpretations — Copenhagen, RQM, QBism, and even Many-Worlds — can be understood as special cases or limiting positions on a deeper epistemological taxonomy. BEI does not compete with these interpretations; it *contextualizes* them by making explicit the epistemological commitments each one tacitly assumes. The paper reframes the interpretation debate from "which interpretation is correct?" to "what epistemic stance does each interpretation embed, and what does the landscape of possible stances look like?"

### Method

Formal philosophical analysis. Construct a two-dimensional taxonomy of QM interpretations: (1) the epistemology axis (realist → anti-realist → relational), (2) the ontology axis (substantival → processual → eliminativist). Position BEI within this taxonomy, then demonstrate how major interpretations occupy specifiable regions of the space defined by these axes. The meta-framework claim is that BEI's epistemology–ontology distinction (derived from the Two Truths doctrine, *dve-satya*) provides the conceptual resources to locate *any* interpretation within the taxonomy.

### Research Gap

QM interpretations are typically compared on physical grounds (which predictions? which modifications to the formalism?). They are rarely compared on the basis of their *epistemological commitments* — what each interpretation presupposes about the nature of knowledge, the role of the observer, and the status of unobserved states. This meta-epistemological angle remains open and underexplored.

### Target Journals

- *British Journal for the Philosophy of Science*
- *Foundations of Physics*
- *Philosophy of Science*
