# Exhaustive Mapping: Buddhist Epistemology and Quantum Measurement
## Ground System: Pramaanavada — Dignaga and Dharmakirti corpus
## Method: Exhaustive tier-by-tier enumeration of Buddhist Epistemology; independent QM query for each concept
## Scope: Epistemology only. No metaphysics, soteriology, or cosmology.

---

# TIER 1 — Sources of Valid Cognition (Pramaana Sources)

### T1.01 — Pramaana (valid cognition / valid epistemic instrument)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The foundational category. A cognition is pramaana if it is non-deceptive (avisamvaadin) with respect to its object and if it is causally efficacious in guiding action. Validity is not intrinsic but relational and functional.
- Quantum Measurement: Measurement operator (Hermitian / self-adjoint operator on Hilbert space). Defines the formal conditions under which a physical result is a valid observable. Validity is defined by mathematical structure, not by relation to an agent.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel with divergence. Both define validity at the level of the act-instrument. Divergence: Pramaana requires a knower; measurement operator does not presuppose one.

### T1.02 — Pratyaksa (direct perception / non-inferential cognition)
- Buddhist Epistemology: One of two valid pramaanas in Dignaga-Dharmakirti. Non-conceptual, non-erroneous, direct apprehension of a particular. Defined negatively: free from kalpanaa (conceptual construction) and abhranta (non-erroneous).
- Quantum Measurement: Eigenvalue readout. The singular, definite numerical outcome of a projective measurement. Non-inferential in the sense that it is the terminal output, not derived from other outcomes.
- Correspondence type: Strong parallel. Both are the terminal non-inferential epistemic event. Divergence: Pratyaksa is non-conceptual by definition; eigenvalue readout is already a number — a symbolic, conceptual object.

### T1.03 — Anumaana (inference / inferential cognition)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Second valid pramaana. Valid cognition of an object not directly present, derived from a logical mark (linga) via invariant pervasion (vyaapti). Applies to objects beyond the reach of pratyaksa.
- Quantum Measurement: Wave function psi. Encodes probabilistic structure of possible outcomes without directly representing any particular outcome. All predictions derived from psi are inferential relative to actual measurement.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel. Both are indirect, mediated, relational representations of what is not directly present.

### T1.04 — Pratyaksa subdivision — Indriyajnaana (sense perception)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Perception arising from the contact of a sense faculty (indriya) with its object. The basic perceptual act. Causally produced by the object via the sense organ.
- Quantum Measurement: Detector response. The physical signal produced in a measurement apparatus upon interaction with a quantum system — photon count, current spike, pointer deflection.
- Correspondence type: Moderate parallel. Both are the causal-physical interface between system and measuring instrument.

### T1.05 — Pratyaksa subdivision — Maanasapratyaksa (mental perception)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Perception arising in manas (mind-faculty) immediately following sense perception. Apprehends the mental image (aakaaara) of the sense object. Still non-conceptual but one step removed from indriyajnaana.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-1 — Post-Detection Internal State. The internal representational state of an observer or apparatus after a detector fires but before any symbolic encoding or recording. QM does not model this stratum. The formalism moves directly from interaction to output value.
- Structural gap: QM has no model of the intermediate representational state between physical detection and symbolic readout.

### T1.06 — Pratyaksa subdivision — Svasamvedana (self-awareness / reflexive cognition)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Every cognition is simultaneously aware of itself. Cognition of blue is also, non-inferentially, an awareness of the cognizing of blue. This is a distinct pramaana for Dharmakirti: self-luminous, not requiring a second-order act.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-2 — Observer Self-Reference. QM has no formal account of an observer's cognition of its own measurement act. The measurement is modeled as a physical interaction; there is no formalism for the system that registers and is aware of having registered.
- Structural gap: This is arguably the deepest asymmetry. Buddhist Epistemology grounds its entire system in svasamvedana. QM has no self-referential epistemic layer.

### T1.07 — Pratyaksa subdivision — Yogijnaana (yogic direct perception)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Direct, non-inferential cognition achieved through sustained meditative cultivation. Accesses objects normally beyond sensory reach. Debated but included in Dharmakirti's framework as a limiting case of pratyaksa.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-3 — Limit-Case Observation. No QM equivalent. There is no formal category for observation that operates by a different faculty or mechanism than standard measurement. All observers are formally equivalent in QM.
- Structural gap: QM has one observer type. Buddhist Epistemology has a spectrum.

### T1.08 — Anumaana subdivision — Svaartha anumaana (inference for oneself)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Inference conducted privately by a cognitive agent for its own epistemic purposes. The full logical structure need not be made explicit. It is the primary form of inference.
- Quantum Measurement: Internal theoretical calculation. A physicist's private derivation of a predicted outcome from psi before performing an experiment. Not formalized as a distinct category in QM.
- Correspondence type: Weak functional parallel. Not a formal QM category.

### T1.09 — Anumaana subdivision — Paraartha anumaana (inference for others / public demonstration)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Inference made explicit in the form of a syllogism (prayoga) for the purpose of convincing another agent. Requires full articulation of paksa, hetu, and drstanta. This is the public, communicable form of inference.
- Quantum Measurement: Published experimental result with full measurement protocol. The public, reproducible form of a quantum measurement, including apparatus specification, procedure, and statistical output.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel at the level of scientific communication norms.

# TIER 2 — Structure of Cognition (Internal Architecture)

### T2.01 — Aakaara (representational form / intentional content)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The form that the object takes within cognition. Cognition does not contact the object directly but apprehends its aakaara. Disputed: Saakaara (with form) schools hold aakaara is real; Nirakaara schools deny it. Dharmakirti holds Saakaara position.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-4 — Measurement Representation. The internal representational structure by which a measurement result is encoded in an observer or apparatus. QM specifies the output value but not the representational form it takes in any cognizing system.
- Structural gap: QM specifies output, not how output is represented. Buddhist Epistemology theorizes the representational structure explicitly.

### T2.02 — Visaya (object domain / intentional object)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The domain to which a cognition is directed. Distinguished from the real object (artha): visaya is the epistemic object; artha is the causal ground. The two need not be identical.
- Quantum Measurement: Observable. The physical quantity to which a measurement is directed (position, momentum, spin). Distinguished from the actual state of the system, which may not have a definite value for that observable.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel with depth. Visaya-artha gap mirrors observable-state gap.

### T2.03 — Phala (result of cognition / epistemic fruit)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The outcome of a valid cognitive act. In Dharmakirti, phala is identified with the cognition itself (pramiti) in its resultant aspect: the knowing, not a separate product. Cognition is its own result.
- Quantum Measurement: Measurement outcome. The registered value after measurement. In some interpretations (relational QM), the outcome is defined only relative to the observer-system relation, not as a separate absolute fact.
- Correspondence type: Moderate parallel. Both resist reifying the result as a separate object from the act.

### T2.04 — Vyavasaaya (determinate judgment / epistemic verdict)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The cognitive act of determining that x is the case: the moment of commitment to a propositional content. Follows pratyaksa but involves conceptual crystallization. It is the point at which cognition becomes actionable belief.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-5 — Measurement Commitment. The moment at which an observer commits to a particular outcome as the result. QM describes the physical process but has no formal account of the epistemic act of commitment to a result as distinct from the physical registration.
- Structural gap: QM collapses the distinction between physical registration and epistemic commitment into a single event.

### T2.05 — Svasamvedana as structural principle (self-certification of cognition)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Beyond being a pramaana type (T1.06), svasamvedana also functions as a structural principle: every valid cognition self-certifies its own occurrence. No regress of meta-cognitions is required. Validity is intrinsic to the act.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-6 — Self-Certifying Measurement. QM has no principle by which a measurement certifies itself as having occurred. In the formalism, measurement is an external imposition on the system. In practice, an experimenter must decide that measurement has occurred — but this decision is outside the formalism.
- Structural gap: QM requires an external meta-level to certify that measurement happened. Buddhist Epistemology does not.

### T2.06 — Kalpanaa (conceptual construction / propositional overlay)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The cognitive act of imposing conceptual, linguistic, and relational structure onto raw perceptual content. Kalpanaa is what turns nirvikalpaka pratyaksa into savikalpaka pratyaksa. It is productive but also the source of most epistemic error.
- Quantum Measurement: Theoretical interpretation. The act of embedding a raw measurement result into a physical theory: assigning it a quantity name, a unit, a place in an equation. This is standard scientific practice but is not formalized within QM itself.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel. Both describe the move from raw perceptual-physical event to structured meaningful content.

### T2.07 — Nirvikalpaka pratyaksa (non-conceptual perception / pre-propositional apprehension)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Perception entirely prior to kalpanaa. Apprehends svalaksana directly. Linguistically ineffable. Epistemically foundational but not propositional. It is real but cannot be stated.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-7 — Pre-Symbolic Physical Event. The quantum interaction between system and apparatus before any numerical, symbolic, or linguistic encoding. QM begins its formalism after this level. The pre-symbolic stratum is outside its scope.
- Structural gap: Identical to BIAN-3 from Tier 1, confirmed here as a structural principle: QM is entirely post-nirvikalpaka in its formalism.

### T2.08 — Savikalpaka pratyaksa (conceptual perception / interpreted apprehension)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Perception already processed through kalpanaa. Carries propositional structure: this is a pot, that is blue. Valid as a secondary cognitive form but not the foundation of pramaana.
- Quantum Measurement: Interpreted measurement result. The eigenvalue after it has been assigned physical meaning: this is 0.5 eV, that is spin-up. The standard output of any real experiment.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel. Both describe the processed, usable, communicable form of a perceptual event.

# TIER 3 — Theory of Objects (Ontology of the Epistemic Object)

### T3.01 — Svalaksana (particular / unique causally efficacious instance)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The real object. Momentary, unique, causally efficacious, and non-repeatable. It is what pratyaksa actually contacts. Its causal efficacy (arthakriyaasakti) is the criterion of its reality.
- Quantum Measurement: Eigenstate post-measurement. The definite, particular state of the system after collapse. Unique to the measurement event. The only real state in the strongest sense.
- Correspondence type: Strong parallel.

### T3.02 — Saamaanyalaksana (universal / conceptual construct without direct causal power)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The conceptual universal. A mental construction abstracted from particulars. Not causally efficacious on its own. Real as a cognitive object but not as a physical particular. It is the object of anumaana, not pratyaksa.
- Quantum Measurement: Superposition / wave function psi. A mathematical construct that represents potential, not actuality. Not directly observable. Causally relevant only through its squared modulus (Born rule), not in itself.
- Correspondence type: Strong parallel.

### T3.03 — Arthakriyaasakti (causal efficacy / power to perform a function)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The criterion of reality. What is real is what can causally produce effects. A universal or concept has no arthakriyaasakti of its own; only svalaksana does. This is Dharmakirti's ontological criterion.
- Quantum Measurement: Observability / empirical accessibility. What is real in QM is what can produce a measurement outcome. Unobservable quantities (hidden variables, absolute phase) have no physical status within the formalism.
- Correspondence type: Strong structural parallel. Both systems use causal-functional efficacy as the criterion of reality.

### T3.04 — Ksamikatva / Ksanikavaada (momentariness / doctrine of momentary existence)
- Buddhist Epistemology: All real entities (svalaksana) exist for only one moment. Persistence is a conceptual overlay (kalpanaa). What appears to persist is a causal series of momentary particulars, not an enduring substance.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-8 — Quantum Temporal Discreteness. QM does not have a doctrine of momentary existence. Time in QM is a continuous parameter. However, each measurement event produces a new eigenstate discontinuously. The between-measurement temporal structure is Schrodinger-continuous; the at-measurement event is discontinuous. This structure is unmapped in QM epistemology.
- Structural gap: QM has the formal structure (discontinuity at measurement) that corresponds to ksanikatva but has not theorized it epistemologically.

### T3.05 — Svabhaava (intrinsic nature — what Pramaanavada denies)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Svabhaava is the concept of an entity having a fixed, intrinsic, context-independent nature. Pramaanavada (following Madhyamaka influence) denies that svalaksana has svabhaava in the metaphysical sense. The particular is real but not essentially self-defined. Its nature is always relational.
- Quantum Measurement: Context-independent hidden state. The assumption that a quantum system has definite pre-measurement values for all observables simultaneously, independent of measurement context. This is precisely what Bell's theorem rules out.
- Correspondence type: Negative structural parallel. Both systems deny the same thing. Bell's theorem is the experimental confirmation of the denial of quantum svabhaava.

### T3.06 — Abhaava (absence / non-entity as epistemic object)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Absence is a real epistemic object. Knowing that the pot is not on the table is a valid cognitive act. Abhaava has its own ontological status as the object of anupalabdhi cognition.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-9 — Formal Absence Cognition. QM has no formal category for cognition of absence. Interaction-free measurement (Elitzur-Vaidman) produces knowledge by non-detection, but this is not theorized as a distinct epistemic category within the formalism.
- Structural gap: QM can perform null measurement; it cannot theorize it as a distinct epistemic object type.

### T3.07 — Samaanya (generic property / repeatable attribute)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Repeatable, shareable properties that multiple particulars can instantiate. Real as cognitive objects; not real as external universals. Instrumentally necessary for communication and inference.
- Quantum Measurement: Quantum number / conserved quantity (spin, charge, isospin). Repeatable properties that multiple particles can instantiate. Treated as real physical properties within the theory.
- Correspondence type: Moderate parallel. Divergence: QM treats quantum numbers as real external properties; Buddhist Epistemology treats saamaanya as cognitive constructs.

### T3.08 — Tadaatmya and Tadutpatti as object-relations (identity and causation between objects)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The two valid relations that can ground inference. Tadaatmya: essential co-nature (smoke and fire share production nature). Tadutpatti: causal production (sprout produced by seed). Both are relations between real particulars, not conceptual connections.
- Quantum Measurement: Entanglement correlations. Quantum entanglement produces invariant correlations between measurement outcomes on spatially separated systems. These correlations are not causal (no signaling) and not identical (no shared substance) — they are a third type of relation with no classical analogue.
- Correspondence type: Partial. Entanglement correlations do not map cleanly onto either tadaatmya or tadutpatti. This may require a BIAN designation.
- BIAN-10 — Non-Classical Correlation Relation. A type of inter-object relation (entanglement) that is neither identity-of-nature nor causal-production. Buddhist Epistemology has no category for this relation type.

# TIER 4 — Error Theory (Epistemology of False Cognition)

### T4.01 — Bhranti (erroneous cognition / illusion)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Cognition that presents its object in a manner that does not correspond to the object's causal structure. Classic example: perceiving a rope as a snake, or perceiving the moon as double. Bhranti is structurally valid (it is a real cognition) but epistemically invalid (it is non-avisamvaadin with respect to its object).
- Quantum Measurement: Measurement error / systematic bias. A measurement that returns an incorrect value due to apparatus malfunction, calibration error, or systematic bias. Real physical process; epistemically invalid result.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel. Both are real cognitive/physical events producing invalid epistemic output.

### T4.02 — Samsaya (doubt / indeterminate cognition)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Samsaya is doubt that motivates pursuit of certainty in pramaana inquiry. In this mapping it functions as a bounded source analogue for structured suspension, not as a binary/equal-weight doctrine or a physical mechanism.
- Quantum Measurement: Pre-measurement registration-state condition. QM describes the system-side state as superposition or density matrix; VVV-QMRF adds the K-side classification of a registering system that encodes alternatives, weights, and coherences before any eigenvalue is registered.
- Correspondence type: Partial. The physical superposition substrate is mapped; the registering system's K-side structured suspension is a derived VVV-QMRF category.
- BIAN-11 — Pre-Measurement Registration Indeterminacy. QM describes physical superposition but does not name the registering system's K-side structured suspension before measurement. The Samsaya link is source lineage only; the VVV-QMRF category remains derived.

### T4.03 — Tarka (counterfactual reasoning / reductio reasoning)
- Buddhist Epistemology: A form of reasoning that establishes a thesis by demonstrating the absurdity of its negation. Not an independent pramaana but a support for anumaana. Used to establish vyaapti (invariant pervasion) by showing what would follow if it did not hold.
- Quantum Measurement: No-go theorems (Bell, Kochen-Specker, PBR). Establish constraints on quantum reality by demonstrating what follows if certain assumptions hold (local realism, context-independence, epistemic interpretation of psi). Prove their conclusions by reductio.
- Correspondence type: Strong structural parallel. Both use reductio methodology to establish foundational invariants.

### T4.04 — Baadhaka pramaana (invalidating cognition / epistemic override)
- Buddhist Epistemology: A subsequent valid cognition that overrides and formally invalidates a prior cognition. The earlier cognition is revealed as bhranti. This is a formal mechanism of epistemic correction built into the system.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-12 — Formal Measurement Invalidation. QM has no formal mechanism by which a subsequent measurement invalidates the epistemic status of a prior measurement. A new measurement updates the state; it does not retroactively address the epistemic status of the prior result. Error correction in quantum error correction codes is physical, not epistemological.
- Structural gap: QM lacks a formal epistemological theory of measurement invalidation.

### T4.05 — Anadhyavasaaya (non-determination / failure to cognize)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The epistemic state in which an object is present but no determination occurs. Neither valid cognition nor erroneous cognition — simply no cognition. A blank. Distinct from doubt (which is active) and from error (which is a misdetermined cognition).
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-13 — Null Observer Event. The state of an observer for whom a quantum event occurs but produces no registration — a detector that fails to fire for a particle that nevertheless passed through. QM models this as detector inefficiency but does not theorize the epistemic state of an observer who neither detects nor fails to detect, simply does not engage.
- Structural gap: QM has detection efficiency parameters; it does not have an epistemological category for non-engagement.

### T4.06 — Smriti (memory / retained cognition)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Cognition that takes a previously experienced object as its content. Not a valid pramaana (it does not produce new knowledge) but epistemically relevant as the basis for inference and as a source of error when confused with fresh pratyaksa.
- Quantum Measurement: Classical record / measurement record. The stored output of a prior measurement. Not a new measurement but the retained result. Plays a role in sequential measurement protocols and in quantum state tomography.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel. Both are retained, non-fresh epistemic content. Both are epistemically secondary.

### T4.07 — Vikalpa (conceptual fiction / purely conceptual construct with no corresponding particular)
- Buddhist Epistemology: A concept that has no corresponding svalaksana. It refers only to saamaanyalaksana with no grounding particular. Example: a rabbit's horn. Epistemically empty in terms of arthakriyaasakti.
- Quantum Measurement: Unmeasurable theoretical construct. A quantity that appears in the formalism but produces no observable prediction. Example: the absolute phase of a wave function, or a specific branch in many-worlds interpretation before decoherence.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel. Both are formally well-defined entities that fail the causal-efficacy criterion of reality.

# TIER 5 — Theory of Inference (Anumaana Architecture)

### T5.01 — Linga (logical mark / inferential sign)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The evidence term in an inference. That from which the probandum is inferred. Must satisfy three conditions (trairuupya) to ground valid anumaana. The linga is the epistemically accessible fact from which the inaccessible fact is derived.
- Quantum Measurement: Measurement pointer state. The macroscopic, classically readable state of a measurement apparatus that indicates the quantum outcome. The accessible evidence from which the quantum state is inferred.
- Correspondence type: Strong structural parallel. Both are the accessible indicator of an inaccessible underlying fact.

### T5.02 — Sadhya (probandum / what is to be proved / inferred object)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The fact to be established by inference. What the anumaana aims to cognize. Typically beyond direct perceptual reach. The linga is the evidence; sadhya is the conclusion.
- Quantum Measurement: Pre-measurement quantum state / wave function. What is inferred from measurement outcomes. Not directly accessible; reconstructed from the pattern of results. The target of quantum state tomography.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel.

### T5.03 — Paksa (logical subject / the case under consideration)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The subject of the inference: the particular entity about which the inference is made. The hill, in the classic inference: the hill has fire because it has smoke.
- Quantum Measurement: Quantum system under measurement. The specific physical system about which the measurement statement is made.
- Correspondence type: Direct parallel.

### T5.04 — Sapaksa (positive confirming case / similar instance)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Cases known to have both linga and sadhya. Establishes the positive correlation required by vyaapti. Where there is smoke, there is fire: the kitchen is a sapaksa.
- Quantum Measurement: Confirming experimental runs. Measurement trials that confirm the predicted correlation between preparation and outcome. Establishes the empirical pattern that supports the Born rule in a given experimental context.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel.

### T5.05 — Vipaksa (negative excluding case / dissimilar instance)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Cases known to lack the sadhya. Establishes that linga does not appear without sadhya. The lake is a vipaksa: no fire, and indeed no smoke.
- Quantum Measurement: Disconfirming experimental runs / null results. Measurement trials in which the predicted outcome does not occur. Establishes the boundary of the correlation.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel.

### T5.06 — Trairuupya (three conditions of a valid inferential mark)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The three necessary and sufficient conditions for a linga to ground valid anumaana. (1) Paksa-dharmataa: linga is present in the paksa. (2) Sapaksa-sattvam: linga occurs in at least some sapaksa. (3) Vipaksa-asattvam: linga is absent from all vipaksa. All three must hold for the inference to be valid.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-14 — Tripartite Measurement Validity Conditions. QM has no analogous three-condition formal structure for when a measurement constitutes valid evidence for a theoretical claim. Statistical significance thresholds and Bell inequality violations serve partial functions but are not organized into a unified tripartite validity structure.
- Structural gap: QM lacks a formal unified theory of what constitutes valid inferential evidence from measurement data.

### T5.07 — Vyaapti (invariant pervasion / necessary concomitance)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The invariant relation between linga and sadhya that grounds all anumaana. Wherever linga, there sadhya. It is not merely correlational but nomologically necessary within the domain. Establishing vyaapti is the central problem of inference theory.
- Quantum Measurement: Born rule. The invariant mapping from amplitude to probability: P = |psi|^2. Nomologically necessary within QM. Empirically confirmed to extraordinary precision. Not derived from deeper principles.
- Correspondence type: Strong structural parallel. Both are the axiomatic, underived invariant relation that makes prediction possible.

### T5.08 — Kevalanvaayin (mark present in all cases / universal positive mark)
- Buddhist Epistemology: A linga that has no vipaksa — it is present in every case without exception. Example: knowable, because everything that exists is knowable. Such a mark cannot use vipaksa to establish vyaapti and requires special treatment.
- Quantum Measurement: Universal conservation laws. Quantities conserved in every quantum interaction without exception (energy, momentum, charge). No experiment constitutes a vipaksa for these laws.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel at the level of universality.

### T5.09 — Kevalavyatirekin (mark present only in negative cases / purely contrastive mark)
- Buddhist Epistemology: A linga established entirely by the absence of the sadhya correlating with the absence of the linga, without any positive sapaksa. Established through contrapositive reasoning only.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-15 — Purely Contrastive Quantum Evidence. No formal QM category for evidence established entirely through null results and contrapositive structure. Interaction-free measurement (Elitzur-Vaidman) comes closest but is not theorized as a distinct evidential category.
- Structural gap: QM has no formal purely-contrastive evidential category.

### T5.10 — Hetvaabaasa (pseudo-mark / fallacious inferential sign)
- Buddhist Epistemology: A linga that appears to satisfy the trairuupya conditions but fails on examination. Dharmakirti identifies multiple subtypes: asiddha (unestablished), viruddha (contrary), anaaikantika (non-exclusive). Each generates a specific type of invalid inference.
- Quantum Measurement: Measurement artifact / confound. A measurement result that appears to confirm a hypothesis but arises from systematic error, apparatus coupling, or selection bias. Analogues to each hetvaabaasa subtype exist in experimental design theory.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel. Divergence: Buddhist Epistemology has a formal taxonomy of fallacy types; experimental physics has informal heuristics.

### T5.11 — Asiddha hetvaabaasa (unestablished mark / linga not present in paksa)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The linga is not actually present in the subject of the inference. The evidential basis is simply absent.
- Quantum Measurement: Null detector / failed apparatus. The measurement apparatus did not actually interact with the system. No valid inference can be drawn.
- Correspondence type: Direct functional parallel.

### T5.12 — Viruddha hetvaabaasa (contrary mark / linga that proves the opposite)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The linga actually establishes the negation of the intended sadhya. The evidence proves the opposite of what was claimed.
- Quantum Measurement: Inverted result / sign error. A measurement result that, due to a calibration inversion, systematically indicates the opposite of the correct outcome.
- Correspondence type: Direct functional parallel.

### T5.13 — Anaaikantika hetvaabaasa (non-exclusive mark / linga present in both sapaksa and vipaksa)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The linga appears in cases with and without the sadhya. The correlation is imperfect; the linga is not a reliable indicator.
- Quantum Measurement: Non-selective measurement / weak correlation. A measurement whose outcomes correlate imperfectly with the target quantum property. Present in both confirming and disconfirming cases.
- Correspondence type: Direct functional parallel.

# TIER 6 — Meta-Epistemology (Epistemology of Epistemology)

### T6.01 — Pramaana-phala identity (cognition is its own result)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Dharmakirti's position: the result (phala) of a cognitive act is not a separate product distinct from the act. The knowing is the knowing-of-the-result. No additional act is required to register the result of a cognition. The act is self-completing.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-16 — Measurement Self-Completion. QM does not formally specify whether a measurement is self-completing or requires a subsequent act (registration, recording, consciousness) to complete it. This is the core of the measurement problem. Buddhist Epistemology resolves the analogous problem via pramaana-phala identity. QM has no equivalent resolution.
- Structural gap: This is the meta-epistemological root of the measurement problem.

### T6.02 — Svasamvedana as meta-epistemological principle (self-certification without regress)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Valid cognition does not require a second-order cognition to certify it. This stops the regress: you do not need to know that you know in order to know. The act certifies itself through svasamvedana. This is the foundation of the entire pramaana system.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-17 — Regress-Stopping Principle. QM has no principle that stops the measurement regress. Who measures the measurer? This is the von Neumann chain / Wigner's friend problem. The chain of measurement registrations is formally infinite in QM without an external stopping condition (consciousness, decoherence, many-worlds branching). Buddhist Epistemology stops this regress formally via svasamvedana.
- Structural gap: The deepest meta-epistemological asymmetry. Buddhist Epistemology has a formal regress-stopper; QM does not.

### T6.03 — Svatah pramaanya vs. Paratah pramaanya (intrinsic vs. extrinsic validity)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Debate about whether a cognition's validity is intrinsic (svatah: established by the cognition itself) or extrinsic (paratah: established by a subsequent cognition). Dharmakirti's position is complex: initial validity is intrinsic; invalidity is detected extrinsically via baadhaka pramaana.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-18 — Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic Measurement Validity. QM does not formalize whether a measurement's validity is established by the measurement itself or by a subsequent verification procedure. In practice, experimental physics uses extrinsic verification (cross-checks, repetition, calibration), but this is not integrated into the formalism.
- Structural gap: QM has no formal account of where measurement validity is located.

### T6.04 — Anaatmavaada as epistemological commitment (no-self as structural principle)
- Buddhist Epistemology: The denial of a permanent, unified self-entity (aatman) underlying cognition. The pramaatri is not a substance but a causal series of cognitive events. This is not merely a metaphysical claim; it reshapes the epistemology: the knower is not a fixed point but a process.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-19 — Observer as Process, not Substance. QM's observer is undefined. To the extent it is assumed, it is typically assumed as a classical, unified, persistent agent — the implicit opposite of anaatmavaada. No QM formalism models the observer as a causal series of events rather than a fixed point.
- Structural gap: QM implicitly assumes a substantive observer. Buddhist Epistemology explicitly denies one and builds its system on that denial.

### T6.05 — Apoha theory as meta-semantic principle (meaning through exclusion, not essence)
- Buddhist Epistemology: All conceptual meaning is constituted through exclusion (apoha): a term refers by excluding what it does not refer to, not by picking out a positive essence. This applies at the meta-level: even the term pramaana refers through exclusion of non-pramaana, not through a fixed positive definition.
- Quantum Measurement: Complementarity / mutual exclusion of observables. Bohr's complementarity: conjugate observables (position-momentum, energy-time) are mutually exclusive; the definition of one excludes the simultaneous definite determination of the other. Meaning in QM is partly constituted by exclusion relations between observables.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel at the meta-level. Both ground meaning in exclusion relations rather than positive essences.

### T6.06 — Pramaana as action-guiding (epistemology grounded in praxis)
- Buddhist Epistemology: Valid cognition is ultimately defined by its capacity to guide successful action (arthakriyaa). Epistemology is not merely theoretical; it is grounded in pragmatic efficacy. A cognition that fails to guide action successfully is retroactively invalidated.
- Quantum Measurement: Predictive success as validity criterion. QM's formalism is validated by its extraordinary predictive accuracy. The Born rule is accepted because it guides successful experimental action. This is pragmatic validation in practice.
- Correspondence type: Deep structural parallel at the meta-level. Both systems ultimately ground epistemic validity in practical efficacy, not in correspondence to a mind-independent reality.
