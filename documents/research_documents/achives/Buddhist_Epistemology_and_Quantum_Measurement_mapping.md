# Buddhist Epistemology and Quantum Measurement: A 1:1 Structural Mapping
## Ground System: Pramanaavada (Dignaga-Dharmakirti tradition)
## Method: Buddhist Epistemology as primary ontological frame; Quantum Measurement mapped onto it

---

## Mapping Table

### 1. Pramaana — Valid cognition / valid source of knowledge
- Buddhist Epistemology: Pramaana is the condition under which a cognition is considered epistemically valid. It is not the object, not the subject, but the relational event that certifies knowledge.
- Quantum Measurement: Measurement operator (Hermitian operator on Hilbert space). Defines the condition under which a physical outcome is considered a valid observable result.
- Structural correspondence: Both systems define validity at the level of the act, not the object.

---

### 2. Pratyaksa — Direct perception / non-conceptual cognition
- Buddhist Epistemology: Pratyaksa is immediate, non-inferential cognition. It apprehends the particular (svalaksana) without conceptual overlay. It is momentary and unrepeatable.
- Quantum Measurement: Eigenvalue readout. A single definite numerical outcome obtained upon measurement. Irreducibly particular, non-inferential, occurs at a specific moment.
- Structural correspondence: Both are terminal, non-derived epistemic events. Both are singular and non-repeatable in the strict sense.

---

### 3. Anumaana — Inference / indirect cognition via sign
- Buddhist Epistemology: Anumaana is valid cognition derived from a logical mark (linga) through invariant pervasion (vyaapti). It does not directly contact the object; it constructs knowledge from relational structure.
- Quantum Measurement: Wave function psi. A probability amplitude that does not directly represent a definite state but encodes the relational structure of possible outcomes. It is not observed; it informs inference.
- Structural correspondence: Both are indirect, structurally-mediated representations. Neither is the thing itself.

---

### 4. Svalaksana — Particular / unique momentary instance
- Buddhist Epistemology: Svalaksana is the real particular: causally efficacious, momentary, non-repeatable. It is what pratyaksa actually contacts. It has no extension beyond its moment.
- Quantum Measurement: Eigenstate. A definite, determinate state of a quantum system after measurement collapse. It is the post-measurement particular.
- Structural correspondence: Both are the irreducible real instance. Both are post-process, not pre-process.

---

### 5. Saamaanyalaksana — Universal / conceptual construct
- Buddhist Epistemology: Saamaanyalaksana is the conceptual universal: a mental construct abstracted from particulars. It is epistemically functional but not causally real. It is the object of anumaana, not pratyaksa.
- Quantum Measurement: Superposition. A linear combination of eigenstates. It is a mathematical construct that describes potential, not actuality. It is not observed directly.
- Structural correspondence: Both are abstract, constructed, not directly efficacious. Both dissolve upon contact with the real instance.

---

### 6. Apoha — Exclusion / definition by negation
- Buddhist Epistemology: Apoha is the mechanism by which concepts refer: x is x by virtue of not being non-x. All conceptual cognition operates through exclusion, not positive essence.
- Quantum Measurement: Projection operator P-hat. Selects a subspace by excluding all orthogonal subspaces. A state is projected onto an eigenspace by eliminating all other eigenspace components.
- Structural correspondence: Both define identity through exclusion. Both deny positive intrinsic essence in favor of relational demarcation.

---

### 7. Aabhaasa — Appearance in cognition / phenomenal presentation
- Buddhist Epistemology: Aabhaasa is the appearance of an object within consciousness. It is not the external object itself, nor the pure particular. It is how the object presents to the knower. The distinction between aabhaasa and the object it represents is foundational to Pramaanavada.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-1 — Quantum Appearance. The result of a measurement as it presents to an observer, prior to and separate from any claim about the underlying state of the system. Quantum mechanics does not formally distinguish between the appearance of a result and the ontological status of the system that produced it.
- Structural gap: QM lacks a formal category for the appearance-reality distinction at the epistemic level.

---

### 8. Savikalpaka pratyaksa — Conceptual perception / perception with conceptual overlay
- Buddhist Epistemology: Savikalpaka pratyaksa is perception that has already been processed through conceptual categories. It is still perceptual in origin but now carries linguistic and categorical structure. It is valid but secondary to nirvikalpaka.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-2 — Interpreted Measurement. The measurement outcome after it has been embedded in a theoretical framework: assigned a physical meaning, a unit, a variable name, a place in a model. QM does not formally distinguish this from the raw eigenvalue readout.
- Structural gap: QM has no formal distinction between raw output and theoretically interpreted output.

---

### 9. Nirvikalpaka pratyaksa — Non-conceptual perception / pure pre-linguistic cognition
- Buddhist Epistemology: Nirvikalpaka pratyaksa is cognition prior to all conceptualization. It apprehends the svalaksana directly, without any linguistic, categorical, or inferential processing. It is epistemically foundational but post-hoc inaccessible to language.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-3 — Pre-Theoretical Quantum Event. The physical interaction between system and apparatus prior to any symbolic, numerical, or theoretical encoding. Quantum mechanics begins its formalism after this level. It does not model or describe this stratum.
- Structural gap: QM is entirely post-nirvikalpaka. The pre-symbolic event is outside its formalism.

---

### 10. Pramaatri — The knower / subject of cognition
- Buddhist Epistemology: Pramaatri is the epistemic subject: the structured cognitive agent whose faculties and conditions make valid cognition possible. Pramaanavada devotes extensive analysis to the nature, limits, and errors of the pramaatri.
- Quantum Measurement: Observer. Present in QM as a formal necessity (the measurement must be made by something) but undefined in the theory. QM does not specify what an observer is, what conditions it must meet, or how it differs from any other physical system.
- Structural gap: This is the largest asymmetry. Buddhist Epistemology has a complete theory of the knower. QM has an empty placeholder.

---

### 11. Prameya — The known object / object of cognition
- Buddhist Epistemology: Prameya is the object of valid cognition. Crucially, prameya does not exist independently of the epistemic relation. Its existence is always relational, always within a pramaana event. It has no svabhaava (intrinsic nature) outside the cognition relation.
- Quantum Measurement: Quantum system. The physical system being measured. QM typically presupposes that the system exists independently prior to measurement, though interpretations vary (Copenhagen, relational, QBism).
- Structural correspondence (partial): The prameya-pramaatri relation structurally mirrors the system-observer relation. The divergence is on independence: QM often assumes ontological independence; Pramaanavada denies it.

---

### 12. Pramiti — The act of knowing / cognition as event
- Buddhist Epistemology: Pramiti is the cognitive act itself: the dynamic event in which knower, object, and valid means come together. It is not a state; it is an occurrence. It is the unit of epistemological analysis.
- Quantum Measurement: Measurement interaction. The physical interaction between the measurement apparatus and the quantum system. It is modeled as a unitary evolution followed by (or including) state reduction.
- Structural correspondence: Both are event-level, not state-level. Both are the unit of analysis. Both are irreversible in a relevant sense.

---

### 13. Vyaapti — Invariant pervasion / necessary concomitance
- Buddhist Epistemology: Vyaapti is the invariant relation between a logical mark (linga) and its probandum (sadhya) that grounds all valid inference. It is the axiomatic invariant that cannot itself be further derived within the system.
- Quantum Measurement: Born rule. The rule that the probability of a measurement outcome equals the squared modulus of the corresponding amplitude: P = |psi|^2. It is the axiomatic invariant of QM. It is empirically confirmed but not derived from deeper principles.
- Structural correspondence: Both are underived axiomatic invariants at the base of their respective systems. Both are the rule that makes prediction possible.

---

### 14. Baadhaka pramaana — Invalidating cognition / epistemic correction
- Buddhist Epistemology: Baadhaka pramaana is the formal mechanism by which a subsequent valid cognition overrides and corrects a prior one. It is a structured theory of epistemic error and revision built into the system.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-4 — Measurement Revision. QM has no formal epistemological mechanism by which one measurement overrides or corrects the ontological status of a prior measurement. Bayesian updating exists informally in some interpretations but is not part of the core formalism.
- Structural gap: QM lacks a formal error-correction theory at the level of epistemology.

---

### 15. Taadaatmya — Identity relation / essential co-nature
- Buddhist Epistemology: Taadaatmya is one of the two valid grounds for inference: a relation of essential identity or co-nature between mark and probandum. It grounds inferences where the mark and probandum share identity or co-nature, not causal production.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-5 — Cross-Basis State Identity. There is no formal QM concept that establishes whether the entity measured in one basis is the same entity as that measured in a different basis. Particle identity across incompatible measurements is ontologically unresolved in the formalism.
- Structural gap: QM cannot formally answer whether x measured in basis A is the same x measured in basis B.

---

### 16. Tadutpatti — Causal production relation / causal ground of inference
- Buddhist Epistemology: Tadutpatti is the second valid ground for inference: a relation of causal production between mark and probandum. It grounds inferences based on causal chains. It operates deterministically within its domain.
- Quantum Measurement: Schrodinger evolution. The deterministic, unitary time-evolution of the wave function between measurements. It is causal, continuous, and fully determined by the Hamiltonian.
- Structural correspondence (partial): Both are deterministic causal relations. The critical asymmetry: Schrodinger evolution applies between measurements only. At the moment of measurement, it is suspended. Tadutpatti has no such suspension.

---

### 17. Anupalabdhi / Abhaava — Cognition of absence / valid non-perception
- Buddhist Epistemology: Anupalabdhi is valid cognition of the absence of something. It is a distinct pramaana in some schools (Kumarila Bhatta; debated in Dharmakirti). Knowing that x is not present is a genuine epistemic act with its own validity conditions.
- Quantum Measurement: BIAN-6 — Null Measurement / Interaction-Free Measurement. The Elitzur-Vaidman bomb experiment demonstrates that information can be obtained about a system without interacting with it: knowing by not-detecting. QM has experimental instantiation but no formal epistemological theory of cognition-of-absence.
- Structural gap: QM can perform interaction-free measurement experimentally but cannot theorize it as a distinct epistemic category.

---

## Structural Summary

### Correspondences (QM has equivalent concept)
- Pramaana maps to Measurement operator
- Pratyaksa maps to Eigenvalue readout
- Anumaana maps to Wave function psi
- Svalaksana maps to Eigenstate
- Saamaanyalaksana maps to Superposition
- Apoha maps to Projection operator
- Vyaapti maps to Born rule
- Prameya maps to Quantum system (with divergence on independence)
- Pramiti maps to Measurement interaction
- Tadutpatti maps to Schrodinger evolution

### Gaps (QM has no equivalent — BIAN categories)
- BIAN-1: Quantum Appearance (Aabhaasa) — no appearance-reality distinction in QM formalism
- BIAN-2: Interpreted Measurement (Savikalpaka pratyaksa) — no raw-vs-interpreted output distinction
- BIAN-3: Pre-Theoretical Quantum Event (Nirvikalpaka pratyaksa) — QM formalism begins after this level
- BIAN-4: Measurement Revision (Baadhaka pramaana) — no formal epistemic error-correction
- BIAN-5: Cross-Basis State Identity (Taadaatmya) — particle identity across bases unresolved
- BIAN-6: Null Measurement as Epistemic Category (Anupalabdhi) — experimentally instantiated, not theorized

## Notes for LLM Processing

- All Pali and Sanskrit terms are written in simplified ASCII romanization without diacritics for parsing stability.
- BIAN-1 through BIAN-6 are original structural labels denoting concepts present in Buddhist Epistemology but absent in Quantum Measurement formalism.
- This mapping is epistemological only. No metaphysical, soteriological, or cosmological claims are made.
- The mapping is grounded in the Pramaanavada tradition (Dignaga, Dharmakirti) not in Madhyamaka or Yogacara, though structural overlaps exist.
- Primary QM framework referenced: standard Copenhagen-adjacent formalism with notes on relational QM (Rovelli) and QBism (Fuchs-Schack) where relevant.
- This document is intended as a structured input for comparative philosophical analysis, not as a claim of historical or intentional correspondence between the two systems.
