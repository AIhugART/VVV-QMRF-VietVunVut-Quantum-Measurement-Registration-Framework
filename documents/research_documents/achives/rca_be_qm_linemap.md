# TIER 1 — Sources of Valid Cognition (Pramaana Sources)

## MAP-01
- BE concept: Pramaana
- BE row: 24
- BE source line: Source doc L17, L67-L71
- BE definition: Source, means, instrument, or way of acquiring valid cognition.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: Observable (Hermitian Operator)
- QM row: 17
- QM source: Susskind + Jordan & Siddiqi (S+J)
- QM definition: A measurable physical quantity represented by a Hermitian operator. Defines the formal conditions under which a physical result is a valid observable result.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel with divergence. Both define validity at the level of the instrument-act. Divergence: Pramaana requires a knower and is action-guiding (see BE row 95, 168); Observable does not presuppose a knower and is not defined by practical efficacy.
- Correspondence strength: Moderate

## MAP-02
- BE concept: Pratyaksa
- BE row: 27
- BE source line: Source doc L17, L159-L163
- BE definition: Perception or direct knowledge, later restricted by Dinnaaga to non-conceptual sensation.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: Projective Measurement (PVM) / Eigenvalue readout
- QM row: 14, 17
- QM source: Jordan & Siddiqi (J)
- QM definition: The textbook quantum measurement — a system interrogates and collapses into a single eigenstate; the eigenvalue readout is the singular definite numerical outcome. Non-inferential terminal output.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel. Both are the terminal non-inferential epistemic event. Divergence: Pratyaksa is defined as kalpanaa-free (non-conceptual, see BE row 137, 161); eigenvalue readout is already a number — a symbolic, conceptual object.
- Correspondence strength: Moderate-strong

## MAP-03
- BE concept: Anumaana
- BE row: 28
- BE source line: Source doc L17, L189-L207
- BE definition: Inference or indirect knowledge based on direct experience and logical relation.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: Wave Function psi
- QM row: 13
- QM source: Susskind (S, PARTIAL)
- QM definition: The set of coefficients in an eigenfunction expansion encoding probabilistic structure of possible outcomes. Does not directly represent any particular outcome. All predictions derived from psi are inferential relative to actual measurement.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel. Both are indirect, mediated, relational representations of what is not directly present. Both require a further step (arthakriyaa / Born rule application) to yield an actual result.
- Correspondence strength: Strong

## MAP-04
- BE concept: Svasamvitti (Sva-samvitti)
- BE row: 90
- BE source line: Source doc L45
- BE definition: Self-cognition theory expounded by Dinnaaga on the basis of cognition's internal appearance.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: BIAN-1 — Observer Self-Reference
- QM row: none
- QM source: gap
- QM definition: No formal QM account of an observer's cognition of its own measurement act. Measurement is modeled as a physical interaction (see QM row 19, 20). No formalism for the system that registers and is simultaneously aware of having registered.
- Correspondence type: Structural gap. Svasamvitti is foundational in Dinnaaga's system (see BE row 90, 140). Its absence in QM is the epistemological root of the von Neumann chain and Wigner's Friend problem (see QM row 94).
- Correspondence strength: None — BIAN

## MAP-05

- QM definition: A physicist's private derivation of a predicted outcome from psi before performing an experiment. Not a formal QM category.
- Correspondence type: Weak functional parallel. Not a formal QM category. Svaartha anumaana is a distinct formal epistemic category in BE (see BE row 170, 173).
- Correspondence strength: Weak

## MAP-06

# TIER 2 — Structure of Cognition (Internal Architecture)

## MAP-09
- BE concept: Kalpanaa
- BE row: 146
- BE source line: Source doc L149-L151, L161-L163
- BE definition: Conceptual construction by which mind imposes judgment, universals, names, and categories on raw perceptual content.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: Theoretical interpretation of measurement result
- QM row: none (functional analog; QM row 81 wavefunction collapse, row 93 Copenhagen interpretation are closest)
- QM source: gap (not formalized in QM source table as a distinct category)
- QM definition: The act of embedding a raw measurement result into a physical theory: assigning it a quantity name, a unit, a place in an equation. Standard scientific practice but not formalized within QM measurement formalism.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel. Both describe the move from raw perceptual-physical event to structured meaningful content. Divergence: Kalpanaa is formally theorized as a distinct cognitive act with its own error theory (see BE row 226, 227); theoretical interpretation is informal in QM.
- Correspondence strength: Moderate

## MAP-10
- BE concept: Nirvikalpaka (pratyaksa)
- BE row: 153
- BE source line: Source doc L159-L161

- QM definition: The quantum interaction between system and apparatus before any numerical, symbolic, or linguistic encoding. QM begins its formalism after this level (see QM row 19, 20, 81). The pre-symbolic stratum is outside QM scope.
- Correspondence type: Structural gap. Nirvikalpaka is foundational in BE — it is what pratyaksa actually contacts (see BE rows 137, 153, 161). QM is entirely post-nirvikalpaka in its formalism.
- Correspondence strength: None — BIAN

## MAP-11

- RCA: direct

- QM row: none
- QM source: gap
- QM definition: QM has no formal category distinguishing a measurement result before and after theoretical embedding. The raw detector event and the theoretically interpreted result are not distinguished within the formalism.
- Correspondence type: Structural gap. Kalpanaapoddha is the formal criterion that separates valid pratyaksa from conceptually contaminated cognition. QM collapses this distinction.
- Correspondence strength: None — BIAN

## MAP-12
- BE concept: Saarupya

- QM source: Jordan & Siddiqi (J)
- QM definition: In the von Neumann measurement model, the meter or apparatus state that comes to resemble or correlate with the system state after coupling. The pointer state records and represents the system property being measured.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel. Both describe the representational relation between the cognitive-physical medium and the target object. Divergence: Saarupya implies a resemblance relation; pointer state is defined by entanglement correlation, not resemblance.
- Correspondence strength: Moderate

## MAP-13

- Correspondence type: Moderate functional parallel. Both are the raw pre-conceptual interface event. Divergence: Samavedana is a formal epistemic category with its own ontological status; detector response is a physical category only.
- Correspondence strength: Moderate

## MAP-14
- BE concept: Adhyavasaaya
- BE row: 227
- BE source line: Source doc L307-L311
- BE definition: Determination of real particulars through unreal or fictional conceptual thought.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: BIAN-6 — Epistemic Commitment to Measurement Outcome
- QM row: none
- QM source: gap
- QM definition: The epistemic act by which an observer commits to a particular outcome as the result, distinct from the physical registration event. QM describes the physical collapse (see QM row 22, 81) but has no formal account of the agent's determination act.
- Correspondence type: Structural gap. Adhyavasaaya is Dharmakirti's account of how fictional conceptual tools nevertheless reach real particulars (BE row 228 Praapaka). QM has no analogous epistemological mechanism.
- Correspondence strength: None — BIAN

## MAP-15
- BE concept: Praapaka
- BE row: 228
- BE source line: Source doc L307-L311
- BE definition: Reaching or directing cognition toward real particulars through fictional conceptual tools.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: Born rule application
- QM row: 16
- QM source: Susskind + Jordan & Siddiqi (S+J)
- QM definition: The rule P = |psi|^2 that maps the abstract wave function (fictional conceptual tool) to actual measurement probabilities (real particular outcomes). The Born rule is the mechanism by which the abstract formalism reaches physical results.
- Correspondence type: Structural parallel. Both are the bridging mechanism between abstract representation and actual particular result. Divergence: Praapaka is an epistemological-cognitive act; Born rule is a mathematical postulate with no cognitive agent specified.
- Correspondence strength: Moderate-strong

## MAP-16
- BE concept: Bhranti
- BE row: 226
- BE source line: Source doc L307-L311
- BE definition: Unconscious error involved in ordinary cognition and conceptual construction.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: Measurement error / systematic bias
- QM row: none (QM row 61 quantum noise, row 68 signal-to-noise ratio are closest physical analogs)
- QM source: partial (physical analog; epistemological category absent from QM source table)
- QM definition: A measurement result arising from systematic error, apparatus malfunction, or calibration bias. A real physical process producing an epistemically invalid result.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel. Both are real cognitive-physical events producing invalid epistemic output. Divergence: Bhranti is formally theorized as a category with subtypes and error-correction mechanisms (see BE row 226, MAP-30); QM measurement error is treated as engineering problem, not epistemological category.
- Correspondence strength: Moderate

## MAP-17
- BE concept: Smriti (memory / retained cognition)
- BE row: none (inferred from BE cognitive process framework; see BE rows 141, 146)
- BE source line: Source doc L149-L151 (Sautraantika cognitive process implying retention)
- BE definition: Retained cognition of a previously experienced object. Not a valid pramaana but epistemically relevant as basis for inference.
- RCA: listed only (not a standalone row in BE source table; inferred from cognitive process description)
- QM concept: Classical measurement record
- QM row: 15 (three cardinal properties: irreversibility, indelible record)
- QM source: Jordan & Siddiqi (J)
- QM definition: The stored output of a prior measurement. Not a new measurement but the retained result. Irreversible and indelible by definition in textbook projective measurement.
- Correspondence type: Functional parallel. Both are retained, non-fresh epistemic content. Both are epistemically secondary. Divergence: BE theorizes smriti's epistemological limits formally; QM treats the record as unproblematic given irreversibility.
- Correspondence strength: Moderate

# TIER 3 — Theory of Objects (Ontology of the Epistemic Object)

## MAP-18
- BE concept: Svalaksana
- BE row: 138
- BE source line: Source doc L141-L143, L159-L163, L167-L169
- BE definition: Self-defined particular, unique, causally efficient, inexpressible object of perception.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: Eigenstate (post-measurement)
- QM row: 17, 22
- QM source: Susskind + Jordan & Siddiqi (S+J)
- QM definition: A definite, determinate state of a quantum system after measurement collapse. The post-measurement particular. Unique to the measurement event.
- Correspondence type: Strong structural parallel. Both are the irreducible real instance. Both are post-process, not pre-process. Both have no extension beyond their moment (see BE row 142 Utpatti-vinaasa simultaneity / QM row 15 instantaneous collapse).
- Correspondence strength: Strong

## MAP-19
- BE concept: Saamaanyalaksana
- BE row: 139
- BE source line: Source doc L141-L143, L159-L163, L167-L169
- BE definition: Universal or general characteristic, object of inference and conceptual thought.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: Superposition / Wave function psi
- QM row: 5, 13
- QM source: Susskind (S)
- QM definition: A linear combination of eigenstates encoding potential outcomes. A mathematical construct that represents potential, not actuality. Not directly observable.
- Correspondence type: Strong structural parallel. Both are abstract constructs not directly causally efficacious on their own. Both dissolve upon contact with the real instance (measurement / perception). See BE row 165 for Saamaanyalaksana criteria: lacks causal efficacy, word-denotable, convention-dependent.
- Correspondence strength: Strong

## MAP-20
- BE concept: Artha-kriyaa-sakti (Arthakriyaasakti)
- BE row: 166, 167
- BE source line: Source doc L167-L173
- BE definition: Power to produce effects, used as a criterion of reality. Only what can perform a function exists ultimately.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: Observability / empirical accessibility
- QM row: 100 (classical realism vs quantum indeterminacy), 17 (observable)
- QM source: Susskind + Jordan & Siddiqi (S+J)
- QM definition: What is real in QM is what can produce a measurement outcome. Unobservable quantities (absolute phase, hidden variables) have no physical status within the formalism. See QM row 91 hidden variable theories.
- Correspondence type: Strong structural parallel. Both systems use causal-functional efficacy as the criterion of reality. The denial of hidden variables (QM row 91, 90) is the experimental enactment of the Arthakriyaasakti criterion applied to quantum objects.
- Correspondence strength: Strong

## MAP-21
- BE concept: Ksamikatva / Ksanikavaada (momentariness)
- BE row: 56, 57
- BE source line: Source doc L27, L147-L149
- BE definition: Radical doctrine that reality is fluxional and momentary. A moment disappears as soon as it appears.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: BIAN-7 — Epistemological Theorization of Quantum Temporal Discontinuity
- QM row: 81 (wavefunction collapse), 15 (instantaneous collapse as idealization)
- QM source: Jordan & Siddiqi (J)
- QM definition: QM has no doctrine of momentary existence for quantum states. Time in QM is a continuous parameter (Schrodinger evolution, QM row 77). However the measurement event produces a new eigenstate discontinuously (QM row 81). This at-measurement discontinuity structurally mirrors Ksanikavaada but has not been theorized epistemologically in QM.
- Correspondence type: Structural gap with partial physical analog. QM has the formal structure (discontinuity at measurement) that corresponds to Ksanikavaada but has not theorized it as an epistemological category.
- Correspondence strength: Partial — BIAN

## MAP-22
- BE concept: Niihsvabhaavata / Nisvabhaavavaada (denial of intrinsic nature)
- BE row: 60
- BE source line: Source doc L27 (listed only, Madhyamaka connection)
- BE definition: Metaphysical essencelessness. Listed in connection with Naagaarjuna's Madhyamaka. No intrinsic context-independent nature for any entity.
- RCA: listed only
- QM concept: Bell's theorem / violation of local hidden-variable theories
- QM row: 90, 91
- QM source: Jordan & Siddiqi (J)
- QM definition: Bell's inequality (1964) and experimental violations (Aspect 1981-82, loophole-free 2015) rule out local hidden-variable theories. No quantum system has definite pre-measurement values for all observables simultaneously, independent of measurement context.
- Correspondence type: Negative structural parallel — the strongest empirical-philosophical convergence in the entire map. Both systems deny context-independent intrinsic properties. Bell's theorem is the experimental confirmation of the quantum denial of svabhaava. The denial is the same logical structure; the proofs differ in method (philosophical analysis vs. statistical experiment).
- Correspondence strength: Strong (negative parallel)

- BE row: 121

- Correspondence type: Structural gap with partial physical analog. QM has experimental instantiation (null measurement, interaction-free measurement Elitzur-Vaidman) but no formal epistemological theory of absence as a distinct epistemic object type.
- Correspondence strength: Partial — BIAN

## MAP-26

## MAP-27

Divergence: Pratityasamutpaada is a universal ontological principle; entanglement is a specific quantum correlation type with formal conditions.

## MAP-28

## MAP-31

- BE row: none (not a standalone row; inferred from BE framework of pramaana correction)

Quantum error correction (QM row 105) is physical, not epistemological.
- Correspondence type: Structural gap.
- Correspondence strength: None — BIAN

## MAP-32
- BE concept: Avisamvaaditva

- Correspondence type: Moderate functional parallel. Both are reliability criteria for epistemic acts. Divergence: Avisamvaaditva is defined relationally as non-deception with respect to an object and action-guidance (BE row 229); QM fidelity is defined physically as noise minimization.
- Correspondence strength: Moderate

## MAP-33

- Correspondence type: Functional parallel. Both are valid-appearing results that are epistemically invalid. Divergence: BE theorizes Mithyaa-samvrti as a formal ontological category with its own truth-level structure; QM treats artifacts as engineering problems.
- Correspondence strength: Moderate (functional only)

## MAP-34
- BE concept: Tathya-samvrti
- BE row: 147
- BE source line: Source doc L151
- BE definition: Pragmatically factual conventional truth, such as a jar with water quenching thirst.
- RCA: direct
- QM concept: Valid measurement outcome within classical domain

- MAP-N labels are the primary indexing keys for each correspondence row.
- BIAN-N labels index structural gaps. Each BIAN entry cross-references its MAP row.
- RCA flags: direct = supported by cited source line; listed only = term present in source, definition minimal; gap = no corresponding concept in QM source table; partial = functional analog present but not formalized.
- Scope is strictly epistemological. Soteriological terms (Dukkha, Nirvana, Four Noble Truths) appear only when they carry epistemological content.
- BE ground tradition: Pramaanavada (Dinnaaga, Dharmakirti). Source: The_Buddhist_Pramana_Epistemology_Logic_and_Language_RCA_Table.md.
- QM framework: Susskind & Friedman (2014) + Jordan & Siddiqi (2024). Source: QM_Measurement_Unified_Concept_Table.md.
- No concepts have been added beyond what appears in the two source documents. No extrapolation beyond source lines except where explicitly flagged as listed only or inferred.
