## BIAN-1 — Post-Detection Representational Transition

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM formalism contains no account of the representational transition between physical detection event and symbolic readout.
- **QM state:** Detector registers interaction → eigenvalue appears on display. The intermediate process — how a physical signal becomes a numerical symbol — is not modeled.
- **RCA:** The formalism treats the detector as a black box. Input: quantum state. Output: classical number. The transformation function inside the box is outside the theory's scope.
- **Consequence:** The physical-to-symbolic boundary is the least formalized step in the measurement chain.
- **Severity:** Moderate. Affects interpretation, not prediction.

---

## BIAN-2 — Absence of Self-Referential Epistemic Layer

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM has no formal mechanism by which a measurement act certifies its own occurrence without requiring a second-order measurement.
- **QM state:** Apparatus A measures system S. To confirm that A has registered a result, apparatus B must measure A. To confirm B, apparatus C must measure B. This generates the von Neumann chain — a formally infinite regress with no internal stopping condition.
- **RCA:** The formalism lacks a self-referential epistemic primitive. Every measurement requires external certification. No measurement is self-certifying within the theory.
- **Consequence:** This is the deepest structural gap. The measurement problem exists because QM has no endogenous regress-stopping principle. All proposed solutions (consciousness, many-worlds branching, environmental decoherence) import the stopping condition from outside the formalism.
- **Severity:** Critical. This is the root cause of the measurement problem.

---

## BIAN-3 — No Spectrum of Observer Faculties

- **Axiom:** A4
- **Gap:** QM defines exactly one type of observer interaction. There is no formal category for observation through qualitatively different epistemic faculties.
- **QM state:** Every observer is functionally identical: a system that interacts with a quantum system and registers a classical outcome. No distinction is made between observer types by epistemic capacity, resolution, or mode of access.
- **RCA:** QM defines observation by function (physical interaction + outcome registration), not by faculty. This is a deliberate simplification that enables universality but eliminates the possibility of faculty-dependent epistemic outcomes.
- **Consequence:** Any phenomenon that might depend on observer faculty type is invisible to the formalism.
- **Severity:** Low for physics. High for epistemology of measurement.

---

## BIAN-4 — No Theory of Internal Representational Form

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM specifies measurement outputs (eigenvalues) but has no theory of how those outputs are internally represented within the registering system.
- **QM state:** A measurement produces a number. How that number is encoded, stored, or structured within the apparatus or observer is not part of the formalism. The theory ends at the output value.
- **RCA:** QM is an output theory, not a representation theory. The formalism models what values can appear, with what probabilities, but not how values are carried internally.
- **Consequence:** Questions about the format, structure, or medium of measurement representation cannot be addressed within QM.
- **Severity:** Moderate. Relevant to quantum cognition and quantum information theory.

---

## BIAN-5 — Conflation of Physical Registration and Epistemic Commitment

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM treats physical detection and epistemic commitment as a single event. The formalism does not distinguish between "detector clicked" and "observer judges: result is spin-up."
- **QM state:** In the projection postulate, the physical event (state reduction) and the epistemic act (outcome determination) are simultaneous and identical. There is no formal interval between detection and judgment.
- **RCA:** The collapse postulate merges two conceptually distinct operations: (1) physical state change, (2) epistemic state change. General epistemology distinguishes these; QM does not.
- **Consequence:** The formalism cannot model cases where physical registration occurs but epistemic commitment is delayed, partial, or withheld.
- **Severity:** Moderate. Affects interpretation of delayed-choice experiments.

---

## BIAN-6 — No Self-Certifying Measurement Structure

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM has no formal principle by which a measurement certifies its own completion without requiring an external meta-level registration.
- **QM state:** The question "has the measurement been completed?" requires a second measurement to answer. But the second measurement faces the same question, generating infinite regress.
- **RCA:** Structural variant of BIAN-2 at the operational level. BIAN-2 concerns the epistemic act; BIAN-6 concerns the measurement procedure. Both trace to the same root: absence of self-certification.
- **Consequence:** No measurement in QM is formally self-completing. Every completion claim requires external validation.
- **Severity:** Critical. Directly generates the von Neumann chain problem.

---

## BIAN-7 — No Pre-Symbolic Stratum in Formalism

- **Axiom:** A2
- **Gap:** QM formalism begins at the symbolic level. The pre-symbolic physical event — the raw interaction before it becomes a number — is not represented within the theory.
- **QM state:** Eigenvalues are already symbolic objects (numbers, labels). The physical event that produces the eigenvalue — the actual detector interaction — is outside the formalism. QM operates entirely in the post-symbolic domain.
- **RCA:** QM is a theory of outcomes, not of pre-outcome processes. The boundary between "physical event" and "symbolic outcome" is assumed, not modeled.
- **Consequence:** The theory cannot analyze what happens before symbolization. This is a structural feature, not a deficiency — but it limits epistemic scope.
- **Severity:** Low for physics. High for foundational epistemology.

---

## BIAN-8 — No Epistemic Theory of Temporal Discontinuity

- **Axiom:** A1
- **Gap:** QM has temporal discontinuity as a physical feature (state collapse is instantaneous) but no epistemic theory of why or how discontinuity occurs at the measurement level.
- **QM state:** Between measurements, states evolve continuously (Schrödinger equation, unitary evolution). At measurement, states change discontinuously (projection postulate). This dual temporal structure is postulated, not derived.
- **RCA:** QM treats the continuous/discontinuous duality as a brute fact of the formalism. There is no deeper principle that explains why measurement introduces discontinuity. The Schrödinger equation and the projection postulate are independent postulates with incompatible temporal structures.
- **Consequence:** The "two dynamics" problem: why does nature have two fundamentally different temporal behaviors?
- **Severity:** High. This is a variant of the measurement problem focused on temporal structure.

---

## BIAN-9 — No Formal Category for Cognition of Absence

- **Axiom:** A2
- **Gap:** QM has no formal epistemic category for gaining information from the absence of a detection event.
- **QM state:** The Elitzur-Vaidman interaction-free measurement demonstrates that information can be obtained without physical interaction — "learning from silence." Null-result experiments (e.g., Renninger negative-result experiment) extract information from non-detection. However, QM treats these as standard measurement outcomes within the existing formalism, not as a distinct epistemic category.
- **RCA:** QM has no taxonomy of cognition types. All measurements — whether by detection or by non-detection — are modeled identically as projections onto subspaces. The epistemic distinction between "learning from presence" and "learning from absence" is not formalized.
- **Consequence:** A potentially distinct mode of epistemic access is collapsed into the general measurement framework, obscuring its unique logical structure.
- **Severity:** Moderate. Relevant to interaction-free measurement theory and quantum counterfactuals.

## BIAN-10 — Non-Classical Correlation Without Causal or Identity Basis (Reverse-BIAN)

- **Axiom:** A1
- **Gap direction:** Reverse-BIAN. QM has a structural concept that Buddhist Epistemology lacks.
- **QM state:** Entanglement produces necessary correlations between spatially separated systems that cannot be explained by: (1) shared identity (the systems are distinct), (2) causal interaction (no signal passes between them, Bell inequality violation). This is a third type of necessary connection beyond identity-based and causation-based relations.
- **RCA:** Classical epistemology recognizes two types of necessary connection: identity (A is B) and causation (A produces B). Entanglement is neither. It is a formally verified relation type that has no classical epistemological precedent.
- **Consequence:** This is the only reverse-gap entry where QM formalism exceeds Buddhist Epistemology's canonical relation categories. Any complete epistemology must accommodate this third relation type.
- **Severity:** Critical reverse-gap. Demonstrates that QM extends, not merely challenges, classical epistemic frameworks.

## BIAN-11 — No Formal Category for Pre-Measurement Registration Indeterminacy

- **Axiom:** A2
- **Gap:** QM describes the system-side state before measurement as superposition or density matrix, but it does not name the registering system's K-side structured suspension before eigenvalue registration.
- **QM state:** A system may be represented as |ψ⟩ = Σᵢ cᵢ|λᵢ⟩ or as a density matrix with coherence terms. The registering system has not yet registered which λᵢ obtains; standard QM provides the physical substrate, not a K-side registration-state category.
- **RCA:** QM models physical states, probabilities, and measurement dynamics, but it does not provide a registration-layer taxonomy for structured K-side suspension. Hidden-variable theories serve only as a boundary contrast, not as an equivalent category.
- **Consequence:** Pre-measurement registration-state suspension is outside the standard formalism; VVV-QMRF adds it as a derived category.
- **Severity:** Moderate. Relevant to hidden-variable, QBist, and epistemic interpretations as boundary contrast.

## BIAN-12 — No Formal Mechanism for Epistemic Override

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM has no formal mechanism by which a subsequent measurement epistemically invalidates a prior measurement claim.
- **QM state:** A new measurement updates the quantum state (via projection postulate). But it does not formally invalidate the prior measurement's epistemic status. The prior result remains "what was measured at time t₁" — it is not retroactively classified as erroneous by the formalism.
- **RCA:** QM updates physical states, not epistemic statuses. The concept "measurement #1 was epistemically wrong" has no formal expression in QM. State update ≠ epistemic override.
- **Consequence:** The formalism cannot express: "the earlier measurement was invalid in light of subsequent evidence." This limits the theory's capacity for self-correction at the epistemic level.
- **Severity:** Moderate. Relevant to sequential measurement analysis and error theory.

## BIAN-13 — No Category for Null Observer Event

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM has no epistemic category for the state where an observer is present, the system is present, but no epistemic event occurs.
- **QM state:** QM parameterizes this as "detection efficiency" (η < 1) — a statistical property of the apparatus. It is treated as a technical limitation, not as an epistemically significant state.
- **RCA:** QM treats non-detection as apparatus failure, not as a distinct epistemic condition. The difference between "did not detect" and "detected nothing" is not formalized.
- **Consequence:** The epistemology of non-engagement — what it means for an observer to fail to engage cognitively despite physical proximity — is outside the formalism.
- **Severity:** Low for physics. Moderate for cognitive epistemology.

---

## BIAN-14 — No Unified Validity Conditions for Evidential Inference

- **Axiom:** A1
- **Gap:** QM has no formally unified set of necessary and sufficient conditions for when measurement data constitutes valid evidence for a theoretical inference.
- **QM state:** QM uses multiple independent validity criteria: statistical significance (p-values, sigma levels), Bell inequality violation thresholds, calibration standards, systematic error analysis. These are methodologically sound but formally disjoint. There is no single unified structure: "measurement evidence is valid if and only if conditions C₁, C₂, C₃ are jointly satisfied."
- **RCA:** QM inherits its evidential standards from experimental physics methodology, not from its own formalism. The validity conditions are meta-theoretical, not intra-theoretical.
- **Consequence:** The theory cannot formally distinguish valid from invalid evidential inference using only its own resources.
- **Severity:** Moderate. Affects philosophy of QM evidence.

---

## BIAN-15 — No Formal Category for Purely Contrastive Evidence

- **Axiom:** A4
- **Gap:** QM has no formal category for evidence that is established entirely through negative instances (null results) without any positive confirming instances.
- **QM state:** Experiments like Elitzur-Vaidman interaction-free measurement produce knowledge exclusively from what did not happen. However, QM does not classify this as a structurally distinct evidence type. It is absorbed into the general measurement framework.
- **RCA:** QM lacks an evidence taxonomy. All experimental results — positive detection, negative detection, null result — are treated uniformly as measurement outcomes. The logical structure of "evidence by universal absence" is not distinguished from "evidence by presence."
- **Consequence:** A logically distinct evidence structure is invisible within the formalism.
- **Severity:** Low. Primarily relevant to formal epistemology of evidence.

---

## BIAN-16 — Measurement Self-Completion Undefined

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM does not specify whether a measurement is self-completing (the act of measuring includes its own completion) or requires an external registration step.
- **QM state:** Copenhagen interpretation implies collapse occurs upon measurement — suggesting self-completion. Many-worlds implies no collapse — measurement is never "complete" in a single branch. Decoherence provides effective completion via environmental entanglement but not formal completion. Each interpretation provides a different answer because the formalism itself is silent on this question.
- **RCA:** The formalism does not contain a completion criterion. "When is a measurement finished?" is not answerable from within QM. This is why multiple interpretations exist — they are competing proposals for what the formalism does not specify.
- **Consequence:** The existence of multiple incompatible QM interpretations is a direct consequence of this gap. If measurement self-completion were formally specified, interpretation plurality would collapse.
- **Severity:** Critical. Root structural cause of interpretation plurality.

## BIAN-17 — No Endogenous Regress-Stopping Principle

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM has no principle within its formalism that terminates the measurement regress (von Neumann chain).
- **QM state:** System S interacts with apparatus A₁. A₁'s state is entangled with S. Apparatus A₂ is needed to register A₁'s state. A₂'s state requires A₃. The chain is formally infinite. Every proposed resolution imports a stopping condition from outside the formalism: consciousness (Wigner), universal branching (Everett), environmental decoherence treated as effective collapse (Zurek), agent belief update (QBism/Fuchs-Schack).
- **RCA:** All solutions are exogenous — they inject a stopping principle that is not derived from the Hilbert space formalism, the Schrödinger equation, or the Born rule. The formalism itself generates the regress and cannot stop it.
- **Consequence:** The von Neumann chain is not a technical problem; it is a structural feature of any formalism that lacks an endogenous self-certification principle.
- **Severity:** Critical. This is the formal expression of the measurement problem.

## BIAN-18 — No Theory of Measurement Validity Location

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM does not formalize whether a measurement's validity is established by the measurement itself (intrinsic validity) or by subsequent verification (extrinsic validity).
- **QM state:** In experimental practice, validity is established extrinsically: cross-checks, calibration runs, repetition, statistical analysis, peer review. But none of this is part of the QM formalism. The formalism specifies what outcomes are possible and with what probabilities — it does not specify how to determine whether a particular outcome is valid.
- **RCA:** QM separates formalism (what can happen) from methodology (how to verify what happened). Validity location is a methodological question that the formalism does not address.
- **Consequence:** The theory cannot distinguish internally between a valid measurement and an invalid one. All projections are formally equal.
- **Severity:** Moderate. Affects measurement reliability theory.

---

## BIAN-19 — Observer Assumed as Substance, Not Process

- **Axiom:** A3
- **Gap:** QM implicitly assumes the observer is a persistent, unified, substantial entity that receives and retains measurement results.
- **QM state:** Every QM formulation assumes an observer who: (a) exists before the measurement, (b) persists through the measurement, (c) retains the result after the measurement, (d) is the same entity at all three stages. QBism (Fuchs-Schack) comes closest to process-observer by treating the observer as a Bayesian agent who updates beliefs, but even QBism treats the agent as a unified decision-maker across time.
- **RCA:** QM inherits the classical assumption of observer persistence from classical mechanics. The possibility that the observer is a causal series of momentary events — not a substance but a process — has no formal expression in QM.
- **Consequence:** If the observer is actually a process (a causal chain of discrete events rather than a persistent entity), then QM's implicit assumption introduces a systematic distortion into measurement theory. The observer-as-substance assumption may be the deepest unexamined presupposition in the formalism.
- **Severity:** High. Foundational ontological inversion.

## Notes for LLM Processing

- All 18 QM-side gaps are structural, not empirical. They cannot be resolved by new experimental data; they require formal extension of the measurement axioms.
- Reverse-BIAN-10 is the only reverse-gap entry: QM has a concept (entanglement) that Buddhist Epistemology lacks.
- BIAN-2, BIAN-16, and BIAN-17 form a causal triad: self-reference → self-completion → regress-stopping. Resolving any one partially alleviates the others.
- This document contains no claims about consciousness, phenomenology, or subjective experience. All gaps are analyzed at the level of formal epistemic architecture.
- Scope boundary: this analysis covers standard non-relativistic QM. QFT and quantum gravity may introduce additional or different gaps.
