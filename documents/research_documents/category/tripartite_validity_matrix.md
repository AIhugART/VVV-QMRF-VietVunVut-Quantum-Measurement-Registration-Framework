## 3. Formal Structure / Cấu trúc Hình thức

**English:**
In standard QM, any interaction that entangles a system with a macroscopic body is loosely called a "measurement". The *Tripartite Validity Matrix* imposes three strict filters inspired by Buddhist Logic (*Trairūpya*):
1. **Condition 1: Pakṣadharmatā (Presence in the Subject):** There must exist a non-zero, direct Hamiltonian coupling between the specific observable of the quantum system and the pointer basis of the apparatus. (The detector must actually look at the particle).
2. **Condition 2: Sapakṣasattva (Positive Concomitance):** In calibration trials, when the system is prepared in eigenstate $|\lambda_i\rangle$, the apparatus MUST yield pointer state $|A_i\rangle$ with high fidelity. (The detector must click when the particle is there).
3. **Condition 3: Vipakṣāsattva (Negative Concomitance / Exclusivity):** When the system is in an orthogonal state $|\lambda_j\rangle$ (or absent), the probability of the apparatus yielding $|A_i\rangle$ MUST be strictly zero. (No dark counts; the detector must NEVER click when the particle is NOT there).
