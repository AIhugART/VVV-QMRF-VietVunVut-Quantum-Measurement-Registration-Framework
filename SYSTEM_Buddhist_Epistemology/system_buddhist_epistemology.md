# Buddhist Epistemology — Key Concepts

| No. | Node code | Concept | Pali / Sanskrit | Category | Definition |

| 1 | N_BE_00001 | Valid cognition | Pramāṇa (Skt.) | Foundation | A reliable source of knowledge; the means by which correct cognition is attained. Structured with four integrated components: pramāṇa, pramā, prameya, pramāṇaphala. |
| 2 | N_BE_00002 | Direct perception | Pratyakṣa (Skt.) | Source of knowledge | Immediate cognition through the senses, prior to inference or conceptual overlay. Dignāga defines it as "devoid of mental construction" (kalpanāpodham). |
| 3 | N_BE_00003 | Inference | Anumāna (Skt.) | Source of knowledge | Indirect knowledge obtained through logical reasoning based on a sign (liṅga). Operates on the universal (sāmānyalakṣaṇa). |
| 4 | N_BE_00004 | Testimony | Śabda (Skt.) | Source of knowledge | Trustworthy word or testimony; Dignāga rejects it as an independent pramāṇa, treating it as inference. |
| 5 | N_BE_00005 | Object of cognition | Prameya (Skt.) | Foundation | The reality that serves as the object of valid knowledge. Dignāga maintains exclusive duality: svalakṣaṇa for perception, sāmānyalakṣaṇa for inference. |
| 6 | N_BE_00006 | Erroneous cognition | Bhrānti (Skt.) | Epistemic error | A mistaken apprehension of reality; an inversion of the true nature of an object. E.g., mistaking a mirage for water is mithyā-saṃvṛti. |
| 7 | N_BE_00007 | Doubt | Saṃśaya (Skt.) | Epistemic error | Doubt that motivates pursuit of certainty in pramāṇa inquiry. |
| 8 | N_BE_00008 | Conceptual construction | Vikalpa / Kalpanā (Skt.) | Mental structure | Conceptualized, categorizing cognition that applies names and classes — contrasted with bare perception. Superimposes structures like proper names, genus-words, quality-words, action-words, and substance-words. |
| 9 | N_BE_00009 | Non-conceptual perception | Nirvikalpaka pratyakṣa (Skt.) | Mental structure | Pure, direct perception free from conceptual or linguistic elaboration (nirvikalpaka, avyapadeśya). |
| 10 | N_BE_00010 | Mental consciousness | Mano-vijñāna (Skt.) | Type of consciousness | Mental consciousness in the Yogācāra eight-consciousness model. |
| 11 | N_BE_00011 | Self-awareness | Svasaṃvedana / Sva-saṃvitti (Skt.) | Type of perception | Reflexive self-cognition theory expounded by Dignāga on the basis of cognition's internal appearance. |
| 12 | N_BE_00012 | Transcendental perception | Alaukika perception (Skt.) | Type of perception | Transcendental or meditational perception not involving external objects and sensory faculties. |
| 13 | N_BE_00013 | Particular / Unique mark | Svalakṣaṇa (Skt.) | Ontology–cognition | The individual, unique characteristic of a thing; the object of direct perception. Per Dharmakīrti: has causal efficacy, is specific, not denotable by word, apprehensible independently. |
| 14 | N_BE_00014 | Universal / General mark | Sāmānyalakṣaṇa (Skt.) | Ontology–cognition | A shared, general characteristic; the object of inference and conceptual thought. Per Dharmakīrti: no causal efficacy, common to many things, denotable by word, not apprehensible independently. |
| 15 | N_BE_00015 | Exclusion | Apoha / Anyāpoha (Skt.) | Philosophy of language | Meaning is conveyed by excluding what something is not (e.g., not-non-cow → cow). Dignāga's theory: a word expresses its object through exclusion of the other, just as an inferential mark does. |
| 16 | N_BE_00016 | Sign / Logical reason | Liṅga / Hetu (Skt.) | Logic | The inferential mark or logical reason used to derive a conclusion (e.g., smoke → fire). A property (dharma) located in the property-possessor (dharmin, pakṣa). |
| 17 | N_BE_00017 | Subject / locus | Pakṣa (Skt.) | Logic | The subject or locus of an argument where the reason (hetu) and probandum (sādhya) are located. |
| 18 | N_BE_00018 | Triple-condition syllogism | Trairūpya (Skt.) | Logic | The three conditions for a valid reason (trirūpa-hetu): (i) pakṣadharmatva, (ii) anvaya / sapakṣe sattvam, (iii) vyatireka / vipakṣe 'sattvam. |
| 19 | N_BE_00019 | Pervasion | Vyāpti (Skt.) | Logic | The logical relation of pervasion between probans (hetu) and probandum (sādhya) that guarantees successful inference. Dignāga introduced it; Dharmakīrti later provided universal foundation via svabhāvapratibandha. |
| 20 | N_BE_00020 | Necessary relation | Avinābhāva (Skt.) | Logic | Vasubandhu's principle: "not-being-without" — the relation of indispensability between hetu and sādhya. The precursor to Dignāga's vyāpti. |
| 21 | N_BE_00021 | Essential relation | Svabhāvapratibandha (Skt.) | Logic | Dharmakīrti's innovation providing universal foundation for inference. Two types: tadutpatti (causality) and tādātmya (identity, e.g., vṛkṣa/siṃśapā). |
| 22 | N_BE_00022 | Causal efficacy | Arthakriyā (Skt.) | Ontology–cognition | Double meaning: (1) Ontological — causal efficacy as the criterion of reality; (2) Epistemological — to fulfill a practical purpose (puruṣārthasiddhi). |
| 23 | N_BE_00023 | Ignorance | Avidyā (Skt.) / Avijjā (Pāli) | Epistemic obstacle | Fundamental ignorance that constructs the phenomenal conventional world and causes suffering (duḥkha). It supports self-clinging through satkāyadṛṣṭi, the mistaken belief that aggregates are the locus of an absolute self. |
| 24 | N_BE_00024 | Wisdom | Prajñā (Skt.) / Paññā (Pāli) | Liberation | Penetrating insight into the nature of reality; the path out of ignorance. The third of the three trainings: śīla, samādhi, prajñā. The Buddha is designated pramāṇabhūta. |
| 25 | N_BE_00025 | Emptiness / Essencelessness | Niḥsvabhāvatā / Sarvaśūnyavāda (Skt.) | Ultimate object | Madhyamaka-linked absence of inherent essence in phenomena, used in Nāgārjuna's critique of realism and pramāṇa theories. |
| 26 | N_BE_00026 | Two truths | Dvisatya (Skt.) | Ontology–cognition | Conventional truth (saṃvṛtisat, prajñaptisat) — the conceptually constructed phenomenal world; and ultimate truth (paramārthasat, dravyasat). Rooted in Sautrāntika view (Vasubandhu's Abhidharmakośa-bhāṣya). |
| 27 | N_BE_00027 | Inference for oneself | Svārthānumāna (Skt.) | Logic | Private inferential cognition based on observation of an evidential sign (liṅga) fulfilling the three conditions (trairūpya) through positive and negative concomitance. |
| 28 | N_BE_00028 | Deductive reasoning | Parārthānumāna (Skt.) | Logic | "Inference for others" — syllogistic reasoning communicating new knowledge to another. Taken in a metaphorical sense (upacāra). |
| 29 | N_BE_00029 | Momentariness | Kṣaṇabhaṅgavāda (Skt.) | Ontology–cognition | The Sautrāntika doctrine: "a moment disappears as soon as it appears without duration." Rooted in the Buddha's dynamic law of dependent arising (pratītyasamutpāda). |
| 30 | N_BE_00030 | Buddha as epistemic authority | Pramāṇabhūta (Skt.) | Epistemic authority | The Buddha as possessor of valid method, arguments, meaning, practice, and purpose; his authority is tested like gold, not accepted by faith alone. |
