# SKILL: RCA for Scientific Papers
> Root Cause Analysis protocol for scientific papers, especially revolutionary cross-disciplinary theoretical work.

---

## Core Axiom

**A scientific paper must solve the root cause of a research problem, not decorate the symptom.**

For revolutionary work, the stronger the idea, the stricter the RCA discipline must be. The paper must trace the visible problem back to the hidden assumption that produces it, then state the new principle conservatively.

---

## 1. When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when drafting, reviewing, or revising:

- an abstract
- an introduction
- a literature review
- a conceptual mapping
- a theoretical framework
- a discussion section
- a claim that may appear revolutionary
- any statement connecting Buddhist Epistemology with Quantum Measurement

Do not revise wording before identifying the root cause.

---

## 2. RCA Stack for Scientific Papers

| Layer | Question | Function |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Phenomenon | What is observed? | Empirical or conceptual trigger |
| 2. Proximate Cause | What directly produces the phenomenon? | Surface mechanism |
| 3. Underlying Mechanism | What produces the proximate cause? | Deep explanatory layer |
| 4. Root Cause / New Principle | What assumption must change? | Core contribution |
| 5. Generalization | Where else may this apply? | Scoped implication |

A valid RCA must move through all five layers before making a major claim.

---

## 3. Project-Specific RCA for Buddhist Epistemology and Quantum Measurement

### Central RCA

| Layer | Project application |
|---|---|
| Phenomenon | Quantum Measurement produces definite outcomes from quantum states described by probabilities. |
| Proximate Cause | The difficulty appears at the relation among measurement, observer, state, and outcome. |
| Underlying Mechanism | The ontology of observation is under-specified: the relation between cognition, object, appearance, and valid knowledge remains unclear. |
| Root Cause / New Principle | The framework should not begin by treating observer and object as fully independent entities without examining the conditions of cognition and appearance. |
| Generalization | Buddhist Epistemology may provide an ontological-epistemological framework for reinterpreting the structure of measurement, within strict conceptual boundaries. |

### Mandatory Boundary

This project must not claim that Buddhist Epistemology provides a physical mechanism for wavefunction collapse unless formal model, prediction, and experimental verification are supplied.

Preferred claim:

> This paper does not claim that Buddhist Epistemology provides a physical mechanism for wavefunction collapse. It proposes that Buddhist Epistemology offers an ontological and epistemological framework for reinterpreting the structure of quantum measurement.

---

## 4. Three-Why Minimum

For every major claim, ask at least three backward questions:

```text
Claim:
Symptom:
Why 1: What directly makes this appear?
Why 2: What deeper mechanism produces that?
Why 3: What hidden assumption makes the mechanism possible?
Root cause:
Fix:
Boundary:
Verification:
```

If the root cause cannot be stated in one sentence, the claim is not ready.

---

## 5. RCA Validity Checklist

A scientific RCA is acceptable only if it is:

- **Falsifiable**: it can generate a claim that could be wrong.
- **Parsimonious**: it introduces the fewest new assumptions needed.
- **Predictive**: it clarifies what should be expected under the framework.
- **Reproducible**: another reader can follow the reasoning chain.
- **Correspondent**: it preserves prior successful theory as a limiting case where appropriate.
- **Bounded**: it states what the paper does not claim.
- **Source-grounded**: it distinguishes scholarship, interpretation, analogy, and hypothesis.

---

## 6. Revolutionary Claim Control

Use conservative form for radical content.

Avoid:

- “This overturns quantum mechanics.”
- “Buddhist Epistemology solves Quantum Measurement.”
- “This proves collapse is epistemological.”
- “This is a revolutionary framework.”

Use:

- “This paper proposes...”
- “This framework may clarify...”
- “One interpretation consistent with this mapping is...”
- “Within the limits of the present theoretical analysis...”
- “The present work does not replace quantum mechanics, but examines...”

---

## 7. Claim Ladder

| Claim level | Status | Recommended use |
|---|---|---|
| Analogy | Structural resemblance | Safe |
| Interpretive mapping | Cross-domain reading | Strong for Paper 1 |
| Ontological framework | New explanatory frame | Use with strict definitions |
| Physical model | Formal mechanism | Only if mathematically specified |
| Empirical solution | Experimental replacement | Do not claim without tests |

For the first paper, stay primarily in **interpretive mapping** and **ontological framework**.

---

## 8. RCA Output Template

Use this structure before revising or drafting any major section:

```text
Section / Claim:

1. Phenomenon:
2. Proximate Cause:
3. Underlying Mechanism:
4. Root Cause / New Principle:
5. Generalization:

Category Risk:
- Is this an analogy, interpretation, ontology, physical model, or empirical claim?

Boundary:
- This paper does not claim...
- This paper claims...

Fix:
- What exact sentence, section, mapping, or definition must change?

Verification:
- Which source, node, edge, mapping file, or published document confirms the fix?
```

## 9. Paper Section Guidance

### Abstract

RCA role: compress the research problem without overclaiming.

```text
[X] remains a central problem in [field].
While [prior work] established [accepted result], [specific gap] remains unresolved.
Here we examine [approach].
We find/suggest that [bounded result].
This may clarify [scoped implication].
```

### Introduction

RCA role: move from accepted knowledge to one precise gap.

1. Establish the field problem.
2. Steel-man prior work.
3. Identify the specific unresolved assumption.
4. Introduce the new framework as an extension.
5. State scope and limitation.

### Literature Review

RCA role: separate source-based scholarship from the project’s interpretation.

Every major claim must be labeled as one of:

- established scholarship
- contested scholarship
- project interpretation
- analogy
- hypothesis

### Discussion

RCA role: explain what the framework clarifies and what it does not solve.

1. Restate the root cause.
2. Show how the paper addresses it.
3. Compare with prior frameworks.
4. State limitations before reviewers do.
5. Suggest future formalization or empirical work.

---

## 10. Red Flags

Stop and redo RCA if the draft:

- changes wording but not the underlying assumption
- treats analogy as equivalence
- treats ontology as physics
- claims a solution without a formal mechanism
- attacks prior work instead of extending it
- uses “revolutionary,” “groundbreaking,” or “overturns”
- lacks a “This paper does not claim...” boundary
- cannot recover prior successful theory as a limiting case

---

## 11. Minimum Final Check

Before accepting a scientific paper section, answer:

```text
What is the symptom?
What is the root cause?
What exact assumption changed?
What does the paper claim?
What does the paper not claim?
How is the claim verified?
```

If any answer is unclear, the section is not ready.
